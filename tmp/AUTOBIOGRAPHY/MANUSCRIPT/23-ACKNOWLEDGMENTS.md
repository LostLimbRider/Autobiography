# Acknowledgments

This book would not exist without the people who carried me when I couldn't walk.

To Genie: You are my rock. My anchor. My reason to come home. I don't say it enough, but I mean it every time. I love you.

To my daughters: Watching you grow into the women you've become is the greatest privilege of my life. I am proud of you beyond words.

To my parents: You taught me what it means to keep going. Everything I am started with you.

To the Lost Limb Riders: You showed me that life isn't over when you lose a limb. It's just different. And different can still be beautiful.

To Roxy: Thank you for the butt scratches, the wagging tail, and the unconditional love. You're a good girl.

To every doctor, nurse, physical therapist, and recovery specialist who worked on me: Thank you for not giving up on me when I was ready to give up on myself.

And to everyone who has ever shared their story with me after a speech: You are why I keep telling mine. Keep going. You've got this.

— John Thompson
