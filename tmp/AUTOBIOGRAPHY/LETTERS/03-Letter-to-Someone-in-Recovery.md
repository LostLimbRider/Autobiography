# A Letter to Someone in Recovery

Friend,

I know what it's like to feel like you've lost control. To wake up ashamed. To promise yourself you'll stop, only to find yourself back where you started.

I've been there.

Addiction doesn't care who you are. It doesn't care about your family, your job, your faith. It wants to take everything, and it will keep taking until you stop it.

I stopped. Not because I was strong. Because I was tired of being weak.

The truth is, I couldn't do it alone. I tried. And I failed. The only thing that worked was admitting I needed help. That was the hardest thing I've ever done — harder than losing my leg, harder than cancer. Because it meant admitting I wasn't in control.

But once I admitted it, everything changed.

If you're in recovery right now, or if you're thinking about it, here's what I want you to know: you can do this. One day at a time. One hour at a time if that's what it takes.

Relapse isn't failure. It's a sign that you need more support. Reach out. Talk to someone. Don't try to do it alone.

And when you make it to the other side — and you will — you'll find a life you didn't know was possible. A life where you're free. A life where you can actually be present for the people you love.

I found that life. You can too.

Keep fighting.

— John Thompson

I Can. I Will.
