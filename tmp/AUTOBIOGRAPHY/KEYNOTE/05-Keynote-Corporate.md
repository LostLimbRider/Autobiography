# Keynote Template — Corporate / Business
## Resilience & Leadership
### By John Thompson

**Audience:** Corporate events, leadership conferences, team-building sessions, sales meetings
**Tone:** Professional, motivational, applicable to business
**Runtime:** 25–35 minutes (adjust by trimming sections)

---

### Core Angle

Frame his story through the lens of **resilience, adaptability, and leadership under pressure**. Draw parallels between surviving life's worst and navigating business challenges.

---

### I. Opening (3 min)

"When people find out I'm a speaker, they expect me to start with the leg. I don't. I start with something more dangerous: the moment I realized I was going to fail."

Set up the idea that failure isn't the end — it's part of the process.

---

### II. The Business Parallels (10 min)

Draw direct connections:

- **Illness** → When a project or company faces an unexpected crisis
- **Addiction** → Bad habits in leadership that slowly destroy effectiveness
- **Loss** → Losing key people and having to move forward
- **Heart attack** → When the organization hits a breaking point
- **Cancer** → Long-term challenges that require sustained effort
- **Amputation** → When you have to cut something essential and rebuild

"Every single one of those moments required me to make a choice. And the choice was always the same: adapt or die. That's true in business too."

---

### III. The I Can. I Will. Mindset (7 min)

- How to build resilience in yourself and your team
- The power of refusing to quit
- Leading through uncertainty
- Creating a culture where people get back up

---

### IV. Practical Takeaways (5 min)

1. Action before motivation — start before you're ready
2. Small wins compound — celebrate progress, not just results
3. Community matters — you can't do it alone
4. Humor is a leadership tool — it disarms, connects, and heals

---

### V. Closing (2 min)

"Whatever challenge you're facing in your business or your career — you can get through it. Not because it'll be easy. Because you have more strength than you know. You just haven't needed it yet."

"I Can. I Will."

"Thank you."
