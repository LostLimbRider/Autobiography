## Mission Statement Hierarchy

Lost Limb Riders maintains three tiers of mission language. Each tier serves a specific purpose. All documents must use the tier appropriate to their audience and function.

### Tier 1 — Legal Mission (Bylaws)

**Source:** Corporate Bylaws, Section 2.01  
**Authority:** Highest. Controls all legal, tax, and governance purposes.

> Lost Limb Riders supports amputees, people with disabilities, veterans, first responders, and their families through peer support, education, advocacy, community engagement, family support, adaptive recreation, and motorcycle‑related fellowship.

**Use in:** Bylaws, Articles of Incorporation, IRS filings, legal documents, board resolutions, grant applications where mission language is required by funder.

**Do not alter** without board amendment and legal review.

---

### Tier 2 — Public-Facing Mission

**Source:** This document (Section 2.1)  
**Authority:** Approved public language. Use in all external communications, marketing, website, press, presentations, sponsorship materials, and donor communications.

#### Section 2.1 — Mission Statement

Lost Limb Riders exists to support amputees, limb‑different individuals, and their families through community, mentorship, public awareness, adaptive opportunities, and practical resources—including housing and independence advocacy—that restore hope, rebuild confidence, and remind every person that nobody is left behind and nobody stands alone.

#### Section 2.2 — Vision Statement

Our vision is to become a trusted national model for post‑limb‑loss community support—connecting amputees and families with peer encouragement, accessible programs, public education, and partnerships that help people live boldly, independently, and with renewed purpose.

#### Section 2.3 — Core Values

**Dignity** — Every person deserves to be seen as whole, capable, and worthy of respect.  
**Brotherhood and Sisterhood** — We build real community through consistent presence and honest support.  
**Courage** — We honor every act of progress, whether it happens in public or in private.  
**Service** — We serve with humility and treat service as a privilege.  
**Accountability** — We handle resources responsibly and communicate honestly.  
**Inclusion** — The road is wide enough for all of us.  
**Resilience** — People can rebuild after devastating change.  
**Authenticity** — We tell the truth about hardship and hope.

#### Section 2.4 — Tagline

"Nobody Is Left Behind. Nobody Stands Alone."

**Use in:** Handbooks, presentations, partner materials, all external-facing documents where the public mission is appropriate.

---

### Tier 3 — Operational Mission

**Source:** This document (Section 3.1)  
**Authority:** Operational language for internal program documents, volunteer materials, SOPs, and program manuals.

#### Section 3.1 — Operational Mission Statement

Lost Limb Riders provides peer support, mentorship, community connection, practical resources, and public awareness to amputees, limb‑different individuals, and their families—restoring hope, rebuilding confidence, and ensuring nobody is left behind and nobody stands alone.

**Use in:** Volunteer handbooks, internal SOPs, program manuals, event planning documents, training materials.

## Application Rules

1. **Bylaws always control.** If any document conflicts with the bylaws mission statement, the bylaws prevail.
2. **Use the appropriate tier.** Legal documents use Tier 1. External communications use Tier 2. Internal program documents use Tier 3.
3. **Do not paraphrase the mission** in legal, financial, or grant documents without approval from the board or executive leadership.
4. **New documents** must reference this file as the mission statement authority.
5. **Existing documents** should be updated to use the appropriate tier language during their next review cycle.
