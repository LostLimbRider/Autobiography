# Lost Limb Riders Corporate Bylaws

## Article I — Name, Status, and Principal Office

### Section 1.01 — Name

The name of the corporation is Lost Limb Riders.

### Section 1.02 — Nonprofit Status

Lost Limb Riders is organized and shall be operated as a nonprofit corporation. The corporation intends to qualify, or maintain qualification if already recognized, as a tax‑exempt charitable organization under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code or the corresponding section of any future federal tax code.

### Section 1.03 — Principal Office

The principal office shall be located in Iowa at a location approved by the Board of Directors. The corporation may maintain additional offices, chapters, program sites, mailing addresses, and registered offices as approved by the Board.

### Iowa and Federal Review Note

Counsel should confirm the corporation’s legal name, registered office, registered agent, Iowa filings, charitable registration requirements, and federal tax‑exemption status before adoption.

## Article II — Purpose, Mission, and Powers

### Section 2.01 — Mission

Lost Limb Riders supports amputees, people with disabilities, veterans, first responders, and their families through peer support, education, advocacy, community engagement, family support, adaptive recreation, and motorcycle‑related fellowship.

### Section 2.02 — Charitable and Educational Purpose

The corporation is organized exclusively for charitable and educational purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3). The corporation shall not carry on activities that are not permitted to be carried on by an organization exempt under Section 501(c)(3).

### Section 2.03 — Powers

The corporation may exercise all powers available to an Iowa nonprofit corporation, subject to its Articles of Incorporation, these Bylaws, adopted policies, donor restrictions, and applicable law. These powers include receiving donations, making grants consistent with mission, conducting programs, entering contracts, employing staff, purchasing insurance, holding property, creating committees, developing chapters, and taking lawful actions necessary to advance the mission.

### Iowa and Federal Review Note

Counsel should confirm that the purpose and powers language is consistent with the Articles of Incorporation, Iowa Code Chapter 504, and IRS organizational test requirements for charitable organizations.

## Article III — Membership

### Section 3.01 — Non‑Membership Corporation

Lost Limb Riders shall be governed as a non‑membership corporation unless the Board and Articles of Incorporation expressly create statutory voting members. Program participants, volunteers, donors, riders, family members, supporters, advisory board members, and chapter participants may be called members for community purposes, but they shall not have statutory voting rights unless the Board formally amends governing documents to create such rights.

### Section 3.02 — Community Participants

The Board may establish classes of supporters, participants, riders, volunteers, donors, and chapter affiliates. These community designations do not create ownership, voting rights, fiduciary rights, or authority to bind the corporation.

### Iowa Review Note

Counsel should confirm that non‑membership status is consistent with the Articles of Incorporation and Iowa law. If statutory members are later created, the bylaws must be amended before implementation.

## Article IV — Board of Directors

### Section 4.01 — Authority

The Board of Directors is the governing body of Lost Limb Riders. The Board holds ultimate authority for mission, strategy, financial stewardship, legal compliance, executive accountability, risk oversight, policy adoption, and protection of charitable assets.

### Section 4.02 — Composition

The Board shall have not fewer than five and not more than eleven voting directors. The recommended launch size is seven voting directors.

### Section 4.03 — Qualifications

Directors must be at least eighteen years old, support the mission, act independently, complete orientation, sign the Board Member Agreement, disclose conflicts, maintain confidentiality, and comply with adopted policies. The Board should seek directors with lived experience with limb loss or disability, nonprofit governance, finance, healthcare, rehabilitation, veterans services, first responder services, legal, fundraising, safety, risk management, community leadership, or motorcycle safety experience.

### Section 4.04 — Election

Directors shall be elected by majority vote of the current voting directors at the annual meeting or at another meeting properly noticed for director election. The Governance Committee shall manage nominations and confirm eligibility before presentation to the Board.

### Section 4.05 — Terms

Directors shall serve two‑year terms unless otherwise specified at election. Terms should be staggered to preserve continuity. A director may serve up to three consecutive full terms. The Board may approve an exception when continuity, specialized expertise, or founder transition requires it.

### Section 4.06 — Vacancies

A vacancy may be filled by majority vote of the remaining directors. A director elected to fill a vacancy serves the remainder of the unexpired term unless the Board specifies a shorter transitional term.

### Section 4.07 — Resignation

A director may resign by written notice to the Chairperson or Secretary. Resignation is effective upon receipt unless the notice states a later date.

### Section 4.08 — Removal

A director may be removed for cause by two‑thirds vote of the current directors after notice and an opportunity to respond. Cause includes failure to attend meetings, breach of fiduciary duty, violation of policy, undisclosed conflict, misconduct, harassment, discrimination, misuse of assets, criminal conduct affecting the organization, or behavior that materially harms the mission.

### Iowa Review Note

Counsel should confirm director number, terms, removal procedures, notice requirements, and voting thresholds under Iowa nonprofit corporation law and the Articles of Incorporation.

## Article V — Officers and Executive Leadership

### Section 5.01 — Officers

The officers are Chairperson, Vice Chairperson, Secretary, Treasurer, and any additional officers approved by the Board.

### Section 5.02 — Election and Term

Officers shall be elected annually by the Board and may be reelected. Officers serve until a successor is elected, resignation, removal, or expiration of term.

### Section 5.03 — Chairperson

The Chairperson leads the Board, presides at meetings, supports agenda development, ensures committee accountability, coordinates executive evaluation, and represents the Board when authorized. The Chairperson does not manage daily operations unless separately appointed to an executive role by the Board.

### Section 5.04 — Vice Chairperson

The Vice Chairperson supports the Chairperson, performs Chairperson duties when needed, leads special governance assignments, and supports board recruitment and accountability.

### Section 5.05 — Secretary

The Secretary oversees notices, minutes, resolutions, corporate records, policy archives, board rosters, attendance records, and governance documentation.

### Section 5.06 — Treasurer

The Treasurer oversees financial governance, chairs or supports the Finance Committee, reviews financial reports, monitors internal controls, supports budget development, and reports financial matters to the Board.

### Section 5.07 — Executive Director

The Executive Director, if appointed, manages daily operations under Board authority. The Executive Director hires and supervises staff within approved budgets, implements programs, manages partnerships, supports fundraising, reports to the Board, and executes policies.

### Section 5.08 — Founder and Chief Executive Officer Role

The Founder/CEO role recognizes the founding leadership, public voice, relationship history, and mission stewardship of Lost Limb Riders. If the Founder/CEO is an employee or contracted executive, the role reports to the Board through an approved position description, compensation arrangement, conflict review, and annual evaluation. If the Founder/CEO is a board officer, board fiduciary duties control. The title does not create ownership of the nonprofit, unilateral authority over charitable assets, or authority to override Board decisions.

### Iowa and Federal Review Note

Counsel and a tax advisor should review officer structure, executive compensation, founder role, private benefit limits, and any related‑party arrangements.

## Article VI — Committees and Advisory Bodies

### Section 6.01 — Standing Committees

Standing committees are Executive, Finance, Governance, Audit when applicable, Fundraising, Programs, Safety and Risk, and Membership.

### Section 6.02 — Temporary Committees

The Board may create temporary committees or task forces for defined projects. Temporary committees expire when their assignment is complete unless renewed by the Board.

### Section 6.03 — Committee Authority

Committees advise the Board unless authority is expressly delegated by Board resolution, these Bylaws, or a committee charter. No committee may amend bylaws, approve dissolution, remove directors, approve merger, approve annual budgets unless delegated by law and policy, or take actions reserved to the Board.

### Section 6.04 — Executive Committee

The Executive Committee may act between Board meetings only on urgent matters within authority delegated by the Board. All Executive Committee actions must be reported to the Board at the next meeting and recorded in minutes. The Executive Committee may not replace full Board oversight.

### Section 6.05 — Advisory Board

The Board may appoint an Advisory Board of non‑voting advisors, including amputees, veterans, first responders, clinicians, safety experts, donors, community leaders, and family advocates. Advisory Board members provide advice, connections, and subject‑matter insight but do not vote as directors and do not bind the corporation.

### Iowa Review Note

Counsel should confirm committee delegation limits and any statutory restrictions on executive committee authority.

## Article VII — Meetings, Notice, Quorum, and Voting

### Section 7.01 — Regular Meetings

The Board shall meet at least quarterly. Monthly meetings are recommended during launch, major expansion, capital campaigns, or periods of increased risk.

### Section 7.02 — Annual Meeting

The annual meeting shall include election of directors and officers as needed, approval of strategic priorities, review of annual financial reports, committee appointments, policy review calendar, and conflict disclosure renewal.

### Section 7.03 — Special Meetings

Special meetings may be called by the Chairperson, Executive Committee, or any three voting directors. Notice must state the date, time, location or electronic platform, and purpose.

### Section 7.04 — Notice

Regular meeting materials should be provided at least five calendar days in advance. Special meeting notice should be provided at least forty‑eight hours in advance when practicable. Emergency meetings may be held with shorter notice when delay would materially harm the organization.

### Section 7.05 — Quorum

A majority of current voting directors constitutes quorum.

### Section 7.06 — Voting

Each director has one vote. Routine actions require majority vote of directors present after quorum is established. Major actions require two‑thirds vote of current directors unless a higher threshold is required by law or the Articles of Incorporation. Major actions include bylaw amendments, merger, dissolution, removal of directors, hiring or removal of the Executive Director, annual budget approval, real estate transactions, loans, chapter charter revocation, and obligations over thresholds set by policy.

### Section 7.07 — Electronic Meetings

The Board may meet by telephone, video conference, or other electronic means if all participating directors can hear one another or communicate substantially concurrently, participate in discussion, and vote in a manner that can be recorded.

### Section 7.08 — Action Without Meeting

Action without a meeting may be taken only when permitted by Iowa law and the Articles of Incorporation. Written consents shall be filed with corporate records.

### Iowa Review Note

Counsel should confirm notice, quorum, electronic meeting, written consent, and voting procedures under Iowa law.

## Article VIII — Conflicts of Interest and Ethics

Directors, officers, committee members, employees, and key volunteers shall comply with the Conflict of Interest Policy, Code of Ethics, Board Code of Conduct, Confidentiality Policy, and Whistleblower Policy. Conflicted persons must disclose conflicts before discussion or action, abstain from voting, and leave discussion when directed by the disinterested decision makers.

### Federal Review Note

Counsel and tax advisors should review conflict procedures for consistency with IRS expectations, private inurement restrictions, excess benefit rules, and Form 990 disclosure practices.

## Article IX — Financial Oversight

### Section 9.01 — Fiduciary Responsibility

The Board is responsible for financial stewardship, annual budget approval, review of financial reports, protection of restricted funds, internal controls, insurance, fundraising accountability, and charitable asset protection.

### Section 9.02 — Fiscal Year

The fiscal year shall be January 1 through December 31 unless changed by Board resolution.

### Section 9.03 — Internal Controls

No one person shall have sole authority over authorization, custody, recordkeeping, and reconciliation of funds. Check signing, electronic payments, reimbursements, credit cards, and contracts shall follow Board‑approved financial procedures.

### Section 9.04 — Audit or Financial Review

The Board shall determine annually whether an audit, review, compilation, or internal financial review is appropriate based on revenue, grant requirements, lender requirements, donor restrictions, risk, and legal requirements.

### Iowa and Federal Review Note

A CPA should confirm audit thresholds, reporting obligations, charitable solicitation filings, tax filings, payroll requirements, and donor acknowledgment requirements.

## Article X — Corporate Records

The corporation shall maintain accurate records, including Articles of Incorporation, bylaws, minutes, resolutions, policies, director rosters, conflict disclosures, financial records, tax filings, insurance records, contracts, grant records, donor restrictions, personnel records, program records, and required regulatory filings. Records shall be managed under the Document Retention and Destruction Policy and Records Management Policy.

### Iowa Review Note

Counsel should confirm statutory recordkeeping and inspection rights under Iowa law.

## Article XI — Indemnification and Insurance

To the fullest extent permitted by law, the corporation may indemnify directors, officers, employees, committee members, and authorized agents who act in good faith and in the best interests of the corporation. The corporation shall maintain insurance as determined by the Board, which may include general liability, directors and officers liability, event coverage, volunteer coverage, motorcycle event coverage, cyber coverage, workers compensation, property, and other coverage.

### Iowa and Insurance Review Note

Counsel and insurance professionals should review indemnification language, exclusions, coverage limits, participant releases, ride requirements, and event risk exposures.

## Article XII — Amendments

These Bylaws may be amended by two‑thirds vote of current directors at a meeting with proper notice. Notice must include the proposed amendment or a summary of the proposed amendment. Amendments affecting provisions controlled by the Articles of Incorporation, tax‑exempt status, membership rights, director rights, charitable assets, or Iowa law require legal review before adoption.

### Iowa Review Note

Counsel should confirm amendment procedures and whether any amendments require filing or member approval if statutory members are later created.

## Article XIII — Dissolution

Upon dissolution, assets shall be distributed for one or more exempt purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3), or the corresponding section of any future federal tax code, or shall be distributed to the federal government, or to a state or local government, for a public purpose. No assets shall be distributed to any director, officer, founder, employee, donor, private person, or private entity except as lawful payment of debts, obligations, or fair‑market contractual compensation approved before dissolution.

### Iowa and Federal Review Note

Counsel should confirm dissolution procedures, creditor payment requirements, plan of dissolution, state filings, donor restrictions, and IRS charitable asset dedication requirements.

## Article XIV — Parliamentary Authority

The Board may use the latest edition of Robert’s Rules of Order Newly Revised as a parliamentary guide when not inconsistent with the Articles of Incorporation, these Bylaws, adopted policies, or law. The Board may also use a simplified consensus process for routine governance if minutes clearly record motions, votes, recusals, abstentions, and decisions.

### Review Note

The Board should decide whether formal parliamentary authority is helpful for its size and culture before adoption.
