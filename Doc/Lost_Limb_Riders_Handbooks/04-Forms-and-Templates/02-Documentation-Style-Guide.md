## 3. Participant, Member, and Rider Terminology

| Term | Definition | When to Use |
|------|------------|-------------|
| **Participant** | Any individual receiving services, attending programs, or engaging with Lost Limb Riders programs | Default term for program documentation, intake forms, assessments, outcome tracking |
| **Member** | Community designation for participants, riders, volunteers, families, and supporters who identify with the organization | Use in community-facing materials, handbooks, welcome letters, social media. Do not imply statutory membership rights (see Bylaws Article III) |
| **Rider** | A person who rides or wants to ride motorcycles in connection with Lost Limb Riders events and culture | Use in ride-specific materials, motorcycle event planning, motorcycle cultural context |
| **Amputee** | A person who has undergone amputation | Use in medical, clinical, and factual contexts. Acceptable in all contexts when referring to the condition. |
| **Limb-different individual** | A person with a congenital limb difference or acquired limb variation | Use alongside "amputee" when the full population is being described |
| **Volunteer** | A person who provides unpaid service to the organization | Use in operational documentation, role descriptions, time tracking |
| **Donor** | A person or entity providing financial or in-kind support | Use in fundraising materials, stewardship records, financial documentation |
| **Supporter** | General term for anyone who aligns with the mission | Use in general communications, social media, casual reference |

**Rule:** In formal program documents, use "participants." In community-facing materials, "members" is acceptable. Never use "victims," "patients" (unless in clinical referral context), "sufferers," or "afflicted."

- "Founder" alone may be used in narrative and biographical contexts when referring to John Thompson.

| Term | Text | Context |
|------|------|---------|
| **Motto** | "I Can. I Will." | Personal motto of the founder. Inspirational context, merchandise, personal communications, closing statements. |

## 6. Disability and Limb Loss Terminology

| Preferred | Avoid | Reason |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| Amputee | "Amputated person," "person who got cut" | "Amputee" is the accepted self-identifier in the limb-loss community |
| Limb-different individual | "Deformed," "handicapped," "crippled" | Person-first, respectful, accurate |
| Person with a disability | "Disabled person" (unless used as identity-first by the individual) | Person-first is the organizational standard; respect individual preference |
| Limb loss | "Losing a limb," "lost his/her leg" (in formal docs) | "Limb loss" is the clinical and community standard term |
| Prosthetic limb / prosthetic | "Fake leg," "artificial limb" (in formal docs) | Clinical and respectful |
| Adaptive equipment | "Special equipment," "handicapped gear" | Neutral, accurate |
| Uses a wheelchair / wheelchair user | "Wheelchair-bound," "confined to a wheelchair" | Person-first, accurate (wheelchairs provide freedom, not confinement) |
| Accessible parking / accessible entrance | "Handicapped parking," "handicapped entrance" | Current ADA and community standard |
| Survivor | "Victim" (except in legal/criminal context) | Empowering, accurate for most contexts |

**Additional rules:**
- Do not use language that implies pity, tragedy, or inspiration solely because of disability.
- Do not describe people as "suffering from" limb loss unless quoting a medical source.
- Respect individual identity preferences — some people prefer identity-first language ("amputee" is itself identity-first and is the community standard).
- In the manuscript and personal narrative, use John's natural voice. This style guide applies to organizational documents.

## 7. Document Formatting Standards

| Rule | Standard |
|------|----------|
| **Headings** | Use `# **Title**` format for document titles and chapter headings |
| **Subheadings** | Use `## **Section**` and `### **Subsection**` |
| **Lists** | Use `-` for unordered lists, `1.` for ordered lists |
| **Emphasis** | Use `**bold**` for key terms, definitions, and section labels. Use `_italic_` for document titles and minor emphasis. |
| **Blockquotes** | Use `>` for direct quotes, mission statements when excerpted, and founder statements |
| **Horizontal rules** | Do not use `---` inside chapter content. Acceptable in front matter, title pages, and non-manuscript documents. |
| **Apostrophes and quotes** | Use curly/smart quotes throughout: ' ' " " (never straight ' ") |
| **Em dashes** | Use — (em dash, U+2014) |
| **Non-breaking hyphens** | Use ‑ (U+2011) in compound modifiers: "limb‑loss," "peer‑driven," "well‑being" |
| **File encoding** | UTF-8 only |

## 8. Cross-References and Authority Chains

All organizational documents should identify their place in the authority chain:

| Document Type | Authority Source |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Bylaws | Iowa Code Chapter 504, Articles of Incorporation |
| Governance Policies | Bylaws |
| Committee Charters | Bylaws Article VI, Governance Policies |
| Program Manuals | Bylaws, Governance Framework, DOCUMENT-CONTROLLED-MISSION-STATEMENT.md |
| Public Materials (Handbook, About, Proposal) | DOCUMENT-CONTROLLED-MISSION-STATEMENT.md |
| Manuscript | Author's voice — this style guide does not govern manuscript content |

When a document references governance terms (board size, committees, financial thresholds, policies), it should state that the Bylaws or Governance Framework controls in case of discrepancy.
