# Lost Limb Riders Chapter Development Manual

## Purpose

Chapters allow Lost Limb Riders to grow without losing its soul. This manual creates standards for local groups operating under the national mission, name, safety expectations, and accountability system.

## Chapter Requirements

A chapter must have at least five active members, one approved Chapter Director, one safety lead for ride activity, one administrative contact, proof of training completion, signed affiliation agreement, approved territory, and ability to report activity monthly. Chapters must follow national brand standards, safety standards, confidentiality rules, and financial procedures.

## Chapter Approval Process

Interested organizers submit a chapter interest form, leadership roster, service area, community need statement, proposed activities, partner opportunities, and safety plan. National leadership interviews organizers, reviews risk, verifies alignment, and recommends approval or denial. The Board or delegated Chapter Development Committee grants provisional approval.

## Provisional Chapter Period

New chapters should operate provisionally for six to twelve months. During this period, they must complete training, submit monthly reports, host at least one approved community activity, follow financial rules, and demonstrate safe culture. Full approval requires successful review.

## Chapter Leadership Structure

Each chapter should include Chapter Director, Assistant Chapter Director, Chapter Secretary, Chapter Safety Lead, Membership Lead, Events Lead, and Family Support Liaison when trained. Smaller chapters may combine duties with national approval, but safety and finance duties must remain separated where money is handled.

## Chapter Reporting Requirements

Monthly reports must include membership updates, volunteer hours, events held, rides held, incidents or near misses, participant support contacts, partner contacts, funds raised, expenses, needs, and upcoming activities. Serious incidents must be reported immediately.

## National Organization Standards

Chapters may not create separate legal entities, bank accounts, logos, sponsorship packages, public policy positions, or clinical partnerships without national approval. Chapters must use approved waivers, training, brand materials, and reporting forms.

## Chapter Revocation

National leadership may suspend or revoke a chapter for safety violations, financial misconduct, discrimination, harassment, brand misuse, failure to report, unauthorized commitments, or conduct damaging to the mission. Emergency suspension may occur immediately when safety or legal exposure is present, with board review to follow.

## Expansion Readiness Checklist

Before national expansion accelerates, Lost Limb Riders should have adopted bylaws, insurance coverage, volunteer screening, chapter affiliation agreement, training modules, reporting forms, brand kit, financial controls, and incident response system.


## Board Review Items

The following standards are recommended for immediate use and must be reviewed, amended if needed, and formally adopted by the Board of Directors before legal reliance or public implementation:

* Final state registration requirements and charitable solicitation registrations.  
* Final insurance limits, carrier requirements, and rider eligibility rules.  
* Final background screening vendor and disqualifying criteria.  
* Final financial approval limits after the first annual budget is approved.  
* Final chapter territory map and national affiliation agreement.  

## Legal Notice

This manual is an organizational operating standard, not legal, tax, medical, or insurance advice. Lost Limb Riders should have qualified counsel, a certified public accountant, and a licensed insurance professional review all policies before adoption.
