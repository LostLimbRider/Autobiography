# Keynote Template — Rehab / Recovery Center
## Addiction & Getting Clean
### By John Thompson

**Audience:** Recovery centers, NA/AA meetings, sober living facilities, addiction counseling conferences
**Tone:** Raw, honest, no sugar-coating, hopeful
**Runtime:** 20–30 minutes (adjust by trimming sections)

---

### Core Angle

Highlight **addiction, hitting bottom, and getting clean**. These people are in recovery or considering it. They need to hear from someone who has been where they are and made it out.

---

### I. Opening (3 min)

No humor here. Start real.

"I'm going to tell you something I don't say in every speech. I've been clean for years now. But there was a time when I couldn't imagine a single day without using. I thought the drugs and the alcohol were the only thing holding me together. They were actually tearing me apart. But I didn't see it until I hit rock bottom."

---

### II. The Descent (7 min)

- How it started — the illness, the pain, the first drink/pill
- The lie: "I can stop anytime"
- The progression — how it got worse
- The impact on family — Genie's tears, the distance
- The shame spiral
- Rock bottom — the moment he realized he'd lost himself

"I looked in the mirror and didn't recognize the person looking back. That was the scariest moment of my life. And I had been through some scary shit."

---

### III. The Climb Back (7 min)

- Admitting he couldn't do it alone
- Getting help — whether formal treatment, meetings, or both
- The early days of sobriety — raw, uncomfortable, necessary
- Relapse thoughts and how he handled them
- The role of faith and family

"Sobriety isn't a destination. It's a daily choice. Every single day, you choose. Some days the choice is easy. Some days it's the hardest thing you'll do. You do it anyway."

---

### IV. Life Now (3 min)

- How sobriety changed everything
- The relationships he rebuilt
- The purpose he found
- "I wouldn't be standing here today if I hadn't stopped. I'd be dead."

---

### V. Closing (2 min)

"If you're in the middle of it right now, I want you to hear this: you can stop. You can get clean. It won't be easy, but nothing worth doing ever is. And on the other side of this fight is a life you can't even imagine right now. A good life. A real life."

"Don't quit before you get there."

"I Can. I Will."

"So can you."
