# Lost Limb Riders Administrative Operations Manual

## Purpose

This manual defines the administrative operating system for Lost Limb Riders. It creates clear authority, consistent records, responsible communication, and scalable procedures for local and national operations.

## Executive Leadership Authority

### Founder / CEO

Purpose: Protect the founding vision, public mission, culture, and long‑term direction of Lost Limb Riders.

Responsibilities include mission advocacy, major relationship development, public speaking, board collaboration, strategic planning, and culture protection. The Founder / CEO may approve public mission messaging, represent the organization when authorized, and recommend strategic partnerships. Operational authority must be defined by board resolution and employment or volunteer agreement.

Reports to: Board of Directors.

Success measurements: mission consistency, partnership growth, donor confidence, chapter culture, public credibility, and adherence to board‑approved strategy.

### Executive Director

Purpose: Lead day‑to‑day operations and convert board strategy into action.

Responsibilities include department supervision, annual operating plan development, staff and volunteer leadership, budget implementation, compliance tracking, program oversight, board reporting, partnership execution, and performance management.

Decision authority includes routine operating decisions within approved budget and policy, hiring recommendations, department assignments, vendor recommendations, and emergency operational actions.

Reports to: Board of Directors.

Success measurements: program delivery, compliance, budget adherence, volunteer retention, partnership satisfaction, safety performance, and timely reporting.

### Deputy Executive Director

Purpose: Support executive operations and maintain continuity when the Executive Director is unavailable.

Responsibilities include cross‑department coordination, special projects, chapter support, administrative supervision, policy implementation, and operational troubleshooting.

Reports to: Executive Director.

Success measurements: project completion, department responsiveness, record accuracy, chapter support, and continuity of operations.

## Department Structure

### Administration Department

The Administration Department manages records, calendars, official correspondence, document control, meeting logistics, compliance calendars, and administrative support. Required positions are Director of Administration, Administrative Assistant, and Records Compliance Officer.

### Membership Department

The Membership Department manages member applications, member records, renewals, onboarding, chapter coordination, member communication, and member engagement. Required positions are Membership Director, Membership Coordinator, and Chapter Directors.

### Family Support Initiative Department

The Family Support Initiative Department manages intake, peer support, family advocacy, resource navigation, referrals, crisis escalation, and support documentation. Required positions are Family Support Director, Peer Support Coordinator, Family Advocate, and Crisis Resource Coordinator.

### Outreach Department

The Outreach Department builds relationships with hospitals, rehabilitation centers, prosthetic clinics, veterans organizations, disability organizations, civic groups, and community partners. Required positions are Outreach Director, Hospital Liaison, and Community Relations Coordinator.

### Motorcycle Operations Department

The Motorcycle Operations Department manages ride planning, rider safety, route review, ride briefings, road guard coordination, motorcycle eligibility, and post‑ride reporting. Required positions are Riding Operations Director, Ride Captain, Safety Officer, and Road Guard Team.

### Events Department

The Events Department manages public events, fundraisers, community gatherings, volunteer scheduling, site logistics, permits, and event evaluation. Required positions are Events Director, Event Coordinator, and Volunteer Coordinator.

### Fundraising Department

The Fundraising Department manages donor cultivation, grants, sponsorships, campaigns, donor records, recognition, and fundraising compliance. Required positions are Fundraising Director, Grant Writer, Sponsorship Coordinator, and Donor Relations Manager.

### Finance Department

The Finance Department manages bookkeeping, accounts payable, deposits, purchasing, reports, reconciliations, budgets, and financial documentation. Required positions are Finance Director, Bookkeeper, and Purchasing Officer.

### Marketing Department

The Marketing Department manages brand identity, public communications, website content, media coordination, social media, newsletters, and storytelling standards. Required positions are Communications Director, Social Media Manager, Website Administrator, and Media Coordinator.

### Legal and Risk Management

Legal and Risk Management supports contract review, insurance coordination, risk assessment, safety policy, incident review, legal compliance, and liability reduction. Required positions are Legal Advisor, Insurance/Risk Manager, and Safety Coordinator.

## Administrative Records

Official records must be stored in a secure digital repository with access controls. Required records include board minutes, bylaws, policies, insurance documents, waivers, participant records, volunteer files, donor records, financial reports, contracts, chapter agreements, incident reports, and compliance filings.

## Document Control

Each policy must include title, version date, approving authority, owner, review cycle, and replacement history. Policies should be reviewed annually. Emergency policy changes may be approved by Executive Leadership and ratified at the next board meeting.

## Communication Procedures

Official external communications must be accurate, respectful, mission‑aligned, and approved by the proper department. Sensitive matters involving participants, incidents, legal issues, financial matters, or personnel must be escalated to Executive Leadership before response.

## Performance Expectations

Every leadership role must maintain records, meet deadlines, follow budget limits, report problems early, treat participants with dignity, and support a culture of accountability without ego.


## Board Review Items

The following standards are recommended for immediate use and must be reviewed, amended if needed, and formally adopted by the Board of Directors before legal reliance or public implementation:

* Final state registration requirements and charitable solicitation registrations.  
* Final insurance limits, carrier requirements, and rider eligibility rules.  
* Final background screening vendor and disqualifying criteria.  
* Final financial approval limits after the first annual budget is approved.  
* Final chapter territory map and national affiliation agreement.  

## Legal Notice

This manual is an organizational operating standard, not legal, tax, medical, or insurance advice. Lost Limb Riders should have qualified counsel, a certified public accountant, and a licensed insurance professional review all policies before adoption.
