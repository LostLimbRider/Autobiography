# Lost Limb Riders Position Description Manual

## Purpose

This manual defines every leadership and operating position in the Lost Limb Riders structure. Each description includes purpose, responsibilities, required skills, preferred experience, time commitment, reporting relationship, and success measurements.

## Board Positions

### Chairperson

Purpose: Lead board governance and protect mission accountability. Responsibilities: agenda leadership, meeting facilitation, committee oversight, executive review coordination, governance discipline. Required skills: leadership, judgment, nonprofit ethics, communication. Preferred experience: board service or executive leadership. Time commitment: six to twelve hours monthly. Reports to: Board of Directors. Success measurements: effective meetings, timely decisions, accountable committees, healthy executive relationship.

### Vice Chairperson

Purpose: Support governance continuity. Responsibilities: back up Chairperson, lead special projects, support recruitment, assist committee coordination. Required skills: reliability, facilitation, strategic thinking. Preferred experience: nonprofit or civic leadership. Time commitment: four to eight hours monthly. Reports to: Board of Directors. Success measurements: leadership continuity, completed governance projects, board engagement.

### Secretary

Purpose: Maintain official records. Responsibilities: minutes, notices, resolutions, record archives, policy records. Required skills: accuracy, confidentiality, organization. Preferred experience: records or administrative governance. Time commitment: four to eight hours monthly. Reports to: Board of Directors. Success measurements: complete minutes, accessible records, timely notices.

### Treasurer

Purpose: Provide financial oversight. Responsibilities: report review, budget monitoring, internal control oversight, finance committee leadership. Required skills: financial literacy, integrity, detail orientation. Preferred experience: accounting, bookkeeping, finance, or nonprofit treasury. Time commitment: six to ten hours monthly. Reports to: Board of Directors. Success measurements: accurate reports, timely review, clean controls, board financial confidence.

### At‑Large Director

Purpose: Provide independent oversight and mission support. Responsibilities: committee service, fundraising support, community representation, policy review. Required skills: judgment, mission commitment, teamwork. Preferred experience: healthcare, disability services, safety, law, fundraising, business, recovery, or community leadership. Time commitment: four to eight hours monthly. Reports to: Board of Directors. Success measurements: committee participation, informed voting, mission advancement.

## Executive Leadership

### Founder / CEO

Purpose: Protect founding vision and public mission. Responsibilities: strategic advocacy, major partnerships, public speaking, culture, board collaboration. Required skills: mission credibility, communication, leadership. Preferred experience: lived experience, nonprofit advocacy, public engagement. Time commitment: board approved. Reports to: Board of Directors. Success measurements: mission integrity, partner trust, donor confidence, national visibility.

### Executive Director

Purpose: Manage daily operations. Responsibilities: staff and volunteer leadership, department supervision, budget execution, program accountability, board reporting. Required skills: nonprofit management, supervision, budgeting, communication. Preferred experience: executive nonprofit leadership. Time commitment: full‑time or board approved. Reports to: Board of Directors. Success measurements: operational performance, compliance, safety, budget adherence, program outcomes.

### Deputy Executive Director

Purpose: Support executive operations. Responsibilities: cross‑department coordination, special projects, chapter support, continuity planning. Required skills: project management, communication, operations. Preferred experience: nonprofit operations. Time commitment: part‑time to full‑time. Reports to: Executive Director. Success measurements: completed projects, responsive departments, smooth operations.

## Department Positions

### Director of Administration

Purpose: Lead administrative systems. Responsibilities: records, calendars, document control, compliance tracking, administrative workflows. Required skills: organization, confidentiality, systems thinking. Preferred experience: office management. Time commitment: five to fifteen hours weekly. Reports to: Executive Director. Success measurements: accurate records, timely deadlines, smooth meetings.

### Administrative Assistant

Purpose: Provide administrative support. Responsibilities: scheduling, correspondence, filing, meeting support, supply tracking. Required skills: reliability, communication, basic technology. Preferred experience: administrative support. Time commitment: three to ten hours weekly. Reports to: Director of Administration. Success measurements: timely support, organized files, responsive communication.

### Records Compliance Officer

Purpose: Protect official records and retention standards. Responsibilities: retention schedule, access control, compliance files, annual records review. Required skills: detail orientation, confidentiality, policy compliance. Preferred experience: compliance or records management. Time commitment: two to six hours weekly. Reports to: Director of Administration. Success measurements: complete files, controlled access, successful reviews.

### Membership Director

Purpose: Lead member recruitment and retention. Responsibilities: membership strategy, onboarding, renewals, chapter membership support, member communications. Required skills: relationship building, organization, data accuracy. Preferred experience: membership association work. Time commitment: five to twelve hours weekly. Reports to: Executive Director. Success measurements: membership growth, retention, clean records.

### Membership Coordinator

Purpose: Maintain member services. Responsibilities: applications, welcome messages, roster updates, renewal reminders, member questions. Required skills: customer service, accuracy, confidentiality. Preferred experience: database use. Time commitment: three to eight hours weekly. Reports to: Membership Director. Success measurements: fast processing, accurate rosters, member satisfaction.

### Chapter Director

Purpose: Lead local chapter operations. Responsibilities: local meetings, reporting, volunteer coordination, partner engagement, safety compliance. Required skills: leadership, judgment, communication. Preferred experience: community leadership or riding group leadership. Time commitment: five to fifteen hours weekly. Reports to: Membership Director and national chapter leadership. Success measurements: safe activities, timely reports, healthy local culture.

### Family Support Director

Purpose: Lead participant and family support. Responsibilities: intake oversight, mentor matching, referral system, confidentiality, crisis escalation. Required skills: empathy, boundaries, documentation, trauma awareness. Preferred experience: social services, peer support, healthcare, recovery, or disability support. Time commitment: five to fifteen hours weekly. Reports to: Executive Director. Success measurements: timely follow‑up, safe referrals, participant satisfaction, complete documentation.

### Peer Support Coordinator

Purpose: Manage peer mentors. Responsibilities: mentor recruitment, training, matching, supervision, debriefing. Required skills: lived‑experience respect, boundaries, coaching. Preferred experience: peer support. Time commitment: three to ten hours weekly. Reports to: Family Support Director. Success measurements: trained mentors, safe matches, consistent follow‑up.

### Family Advocate

Purpose: Support families and caregivers. Responsibilities: listening support, resource navigation, caregiver check‑ins, referral documentation. Required skills: compassion, boundaries, confidentiality. Preferred experience: caregiving, social services, healthcare, or recovery support. Time commitment: two to eight hours weekly. Reports to: Family Support Director. Success measurements: family engagement, appropriate referrals, documented contacts.

### Crisis Resource Coordinator

Purpose: Manage crisis escalation and resource pathways. Responsibilities: crisis protocol training, urgent referral coordination, resource list maintenance, incident documentation. Required skills: calm judgment, crisis boundaries, documentation. Preferred experience: crisis line, behavioral health, emergency services, social work, or pastoral care. Time commitment: on‑call schedule as approved. Reports to: Family Support Director. Success measurements: timely escalation, updated resources, complete reports.

### Outreach Director

Purpose: Lead institutional and community partnerships. Responsibilities: outreach strategy, hospital relationships, partner agreements, presentations. Required skills: public speaking, professionalism, follow‑through. Preferred experience: healthcare outreach or community relations. Time commitment: five to twelve hours weekly. Reports to: Executive Director. Success measurements: active partners, referral pathways, successful presentations.

### Hospital Liaison

Purpose: Build hospital and rehabilitation relationships. Responsibilities: facility contact, visit coordination, compliance with facility rules, referral communication. Required skills: professionalism, boundaries, communication. Preferred experience: healthcare setting. Time commitment: three to eight hours weekly. Reports to: Outreach Director. Success measurements: approved facility relationships, safe visits, partner satisfaction.

### Community Relations Coordinator

Purpose: Connect with civic and community groups. Responsibilities: presentations, resource fairs, partner database, follow‑up. Required skills: communication, organization, relationship building. Preferred experience: community outreach. Time commitment: three to eight hours weekly. Reports to: Outreach Director. Success measurements: community contacts, event invitations, partner referrals.

### Riding Operations Director

Purpose: Lead safe motorcycle operations. Responsibilities: ride standards, route approval, ride captain training, safety review. Required skills: motorcycle experience, safety judgment, leadership. Preferred experience: group ride leadership. Time commitment: five to twelve hours weekly during riding season. Reports to: Executive Director. Success measurements: safe rides, trained leaders, complete ride reports.

### Ride Captain

Purpose: Lead individual rides. Responsibilities: route briefing, rider check, pace control, stops, emergency coordination. Required skills: experienced riding, communication, calm judgment. Preferred experience: organized ride leadership. Time commitment: per ride plus preparation. Reports to: Riding Operations Director. Success measurements: safe ride completion, clear briefings, no unmanaged incidents.

### Safety Officer

Purpose: Monitor ride and event safety. Responsibilities: hazard identification, safety briefings, incident reports, corrective recommendations. Required skills: risk awareness, authority to stop unsafe activity, documentation. Preferred experience: safety, EMS, military, or road guard work. Time commitment: per event or ride. Reports to: Riding Operations Director or Safety Coordinator. Success measurements: hazards addressed, reports completed, unsafe activity stopped.

### Road Guard Team

Purpose: Support ride flow where legally permitted. Responsibilities: assigned road positions, communication, rider assistance, hazard alerts. Required skills: riding skill, discipline, local law awareness. Preferred experience: road guard training. Time commitment: per ride. Reports to: Ride Captain. Success measurements: disciplined execution, safety compliance, clear communication.

### Events Director

Purpose: Lead organizational events. Responsibilities: event calendar, budgets, logistics, permits, volunteer plan, evaluation. Required skills: planning, communication, budget awareness. Preferred experience: event management. Time commitment: five to fifteen hours weekly during event cycles. Reports to: Executive Director. Success measurements: safe events, attendance goals, budget adherence, partner satisfaction.

### Event Coordinator

Purpose: Execute event logistics. Responsibilities: venue coordination, supplies, schedules, vendor communication, setup plans. Required skills: organization, problem solving. Preferred experience: event support. Time commitment: per event cycle. Reports to: Events Director. Success measurements: on‑time logistics, complete checklists, smooth event execution.

### Volunteer Coordinator

Purpose: Recruit, schedule, and support volunteers. Responsibilities: applications, orientation, schedules, role assignment, recognition. Required skills: people skills, organization, follow‑up. Preferred experience: volunteer management. Time commitment: three to ten hours weekly. Reports to: Events Director or Director of Administration. Success measurements: filled shifts, trained volunteers, retention.

### Fundraising Director

Purpose: Lead revenue development. Responsibilities: fundraising plan, donor cultivation, campaigns, grant coordination, sponsorship oversight. Required skills: relationship building, ethics, planning. Preferred experience: nonprofit fundraising. Time commitment: five to fifteen hours weekly. Reports to: Executive Director. Success measurements: revenue goals, donor retention, compliant campaigns.

### Grant Writer

Purpose: Develop grant applications and reports. Responsibilities: prospect research, narratives, budgets with Finance, submissions, reporting deadlines. Required skills: writing, research, deadline management. Preferred experience: grant writing. Time commitment: project based. Reports to: Fundraising Director. Success measurements: timely submissions, quality proposals, compliant reports.

### Sponsorship Coordinator

Purpose: Manage corporate sponsors. Responsibilities: sponsor packages, outreach, agreements, benefit fulfillment, renewal tracking. Required skills: sales communication, organization, professionalism. Preferred experience: sponsorship or business development. Time commitment: three to ten hours weekly. Reports to: Fundraising Director. Success measurements: sponsorship revenue, fulfilled benefits, renewals.

### Donor Relations Manager

Purpose: Steward donors. Responsibilities: acknowledgments, donor updates, recognition, donor records, retention outreach. Required skills: gratitude, accuracy, confidentiality. Preferred experience: donor relations. Time commitment: three to eight hours weekly. Reports to: Fundraising Director. Success measurements: timely acknowledgments, donor retention, accurate records.

### Finance Director

Purpose: Lead financial administration. Responsibilities: budget process, reports, internal controls, finance calendar, coordination with Treasurer. Required skills: financial management, integrity, accuracy. Preferred experience: nonprofit finance. Time commitment: five to twelve hours weekly. Reports to: Executive Director with board Treasurer oversight. Success measurements: timely reports, clean records, budget compliance.

### Bookkeeper

Purpose: Maintain financial records. Responsibilities: entries, reconciliations, payable records, receivable records, documentation. Required skills: bookkeeping accuracy, confidentiality. Preferred experience: accounting software. Time commitment: three to ten hours weekly. Reports to: Finance Director. Success measurements: accurate books, timely reconciliations, complete documentation.

### Purchasing Officer

Purpose: Manage purchasing controls. Responsibilities: purchase requests, quote tracking, receipt collection, vendor files. Required skills: organization, policy compliance. Preferred experience: procurement. Time commitment: two to six hours weekly. Reports to: Finance Director. Success measurements: documented purchases, policy compliance, cost control.

### Communications Director

Purpose: Lead public messaging and brand. Responsibilities: communications plan, brand standards, media review, storytelling, crisis communication coordination. Required skills: writing, judgment, brand discipline. Preferred experience: communications or public relations. Time commitment: five to twelve hours weekly. Reports to: Executive Director. Success measurements: consistent messaging, timely content, positive public presence.

### Social Media Manager

Purpose: Manage social platforms. Responsibilities: content calendar, posts, comments, moderation, analytics. Required skills: platform knowledge, judgment, respectful tone. Preferred experience: social media management. Time commitment: three to eight hours weekly. Reports to: Communications Director. Success measurements: consistent posting, safe moderation, engagement growth.

### Website Administrator

Purpose: Maintain website content and functionality. Responsibilities: updates, accessibility checks, forms, event pages, donation links, basic analytics. Required skills: web content management, accuracy. Preferred experience: website platforms. Time commitment: two to six hours weekly. Reports to: Communications Director. Success measurements: current pages, functioning forms, accessible content.

### Media Coordinator

Purpose: Coordinate press and media requests. Responsibilities: media list, press releases, interview scheduling, media kit, story consent verification. Required skills: professionalism, writing, discretion. Preferred experience: media relations. Time commitment: project based. Reports to: Communications Director. Success measurements: accurate media responses, protected participant stories, organized media assets.

### Legal Advisor

Purpose: Advise on legal risk. Responsibilities: review contracts, waivers, policies, compliance questions, governance issues. Required skills: legal expertise and nonprofit awareness. Preferred experience: nonprofit, liability, healthcare, or corporate law. Time commitment: as needed. Reports to: Board or Executive Director as defined. Success measurements: timely review, reduced legal exposure, clear guidance.

### Insurance/Risk Manager

Purpose: Manage insurance and organizational risk records. Responsibilities: coverage review, certificates, claim coordination, risk register, broker communication. Required skills: insurance literacy, detail orientation. Preferred experience: insurance or risk management. Time commitment: two to six hours weekly. Reports to: Legal and Risk Management lead or Executive Director. Success measurements: active coverage, tracked certificates, timely claim reporting.

### Safety Coordinator

Purpose: Coordinate safety standards across programs. Responsibilities: safety training, incident trend review, corrective action tracking, safety checklists. Required skills: risk awareness, training, documentation. Preferred experience: safety, emergency services, motorcycle safety, or event operations. Time commitment: three to eight hours weekly. Reports to: Executive Director or Legal and Risk Management lead. Success measurements: completed training, reduced incidents, documented corrective actions.


## Board Review Items

The following standards are recommended for immediate use and must be reviewed, amended if needed, and formally adopted by the Board of Directors before legal reliance or public implementation:

* Final state registration requirements and charitable solicitation registrations.  
* Final insurance limits, carrier requirements, and rider eligibility rules.  
* Final background screening vendor and disqualifying criteria.  
* Final financial approval limits after the first annual budget is approved.  
* Final chapter territory map and national affiliation agreement.  

## Legal Notice

This manual is an organizational operating standard, not legal, tax, medical, or insurance advice. Lost Limb Riders should have qualified counsel, a certified public accountant, and a licensed insurance professional review all policies before adoption.
