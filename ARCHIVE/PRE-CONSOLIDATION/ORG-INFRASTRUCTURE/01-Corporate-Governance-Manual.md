# Lost Limb Riders Corporate Governance Manual

## Purpose

Lost Limb Riders exists to build community, confidence, practical support, and safe motorcycle fellowship for amputees, limb‑difference individuals, families, caregivers, and allies. This manual establishes the governing system needed for a professional nonprofit capable of local operation, hospital partnership, responsible fundraising, and national expansion.

## Chain of Command

Board of Directors  
Executive Leadership  
Department Directors  
Program Coordinators  
Volunteers and Members

The Board governs mission, fiduciary duty, strategic direction, legal compliance, and executive accountability. Executive Leadership manages daily operations. Department Directors manage assigned operating areas. Program Coordinators execute approved programs. Volunteers and members serve within assigned roles, policies, and training requirements.

## Board Structure

The Board should include five to eleven voting directors. The recommended launch structure is seven voting directors: Chairperson, Vice Chairperson, Secretary, Treasurer, and three At‑Large Directors. A larger national organization may expand to eleven directors by board vote.

At least one director should have lived experience with limb loss or limb difference. At least one director should have finance, accounting, or nonprofit fiscal experience. At least one director should have healthcare, rehabilitation, veterans, disability services, safety, or risk management experience.

## Board Member Requirements

Directors must support the mission, act in the organization’s best interest, maintain confidentiality, disclose conflicts, attend meetings, participate in committees, review materials before votes, and comply with all adopted policies. Directors must be at least eighteen years old and able to exercise independent judgment.

## Officer Responsibilities

### Chairperson

The Chairperson leads board meetings, sets agendas with Executive Leadership, ensures committees function, represents the Board when authorized, conducts annual executive performance review coordination, and protects the integrity of governance. The Chairperson does not manage daily operations unless also formally serving in an executive role approved by the Board.

### Vice Chairperson

The Vice Chairperson supports the Chairperson, performs Chairperson duties when the Chairperson is unavailable, leads special governance projects, and assists with board recruitment and accountability.

### Secretary

The Secretary maintains official minutes, board resolutions, corporate records, director rosters, meeting notices, attendance records, and adopted policy archives. Minutes must record motions, seconds, votes, recusals, abstentions, and action items.

### Treasurer

The Treasurer oversees financial governance, reviews monthly financial reports, chairs or supports the Finance Committee, confirms internal controls are followed, and presents financial information to the Board. The Treasurer does not serve as the sole person with custody, approval, and reconciliation authority over funds.

### At‑Large Directors

At‑Large Directors provide independent oversight, committee leadership, community representation, fundraising support, and strategic guidance.

## Election Process and Terms

Directors are elected by majority vote of seated directors unless bylaws later provide member voting rights. Initial directors should serve staggered terms so continuity is protected. Recommended terms are two years, renewable for up to three consecutive terms. After six consecutive years, a director should rotate off for at least one year unless the Board approves an exception for continuity.

Officer elections should occur annually at the first regular meeting of the fiscal year. Vacancies may be filled by majority vote for the remainder of the term.

## Voting Procedures

A quorum is a majority of current voting directors. Each director has one vote. Routine motions pass by majority vote of directors present once quorum is established. Major actions require two‑thirds approval of current directors: bylaw amendments, executive hiring or removal, annual budget approval, merger, dissolution, chapter revocation, real estate obligations, loans, and commitments over board‑approved thresholds.

Directors with conflicts must disclose the conflict, leave discussion when directed, and abstain from voting. The minutes must document the disclosure and abstention.

## Meeting Procedures

The Board should meet at least quarterly. Monthly meetings are recommended during the launch phase. Agendas should be distributed at least five calendar days before regular meetings. Emergency meetings may be called by the Chairperson or any three directors with reasonable notice.

Meetings should include mission moment, approval of prior minutes, financial report, executive report, committee reports, old business, new business, action items, and executive session when needed.

## Committee Structure

Standing committees should include Governance, Finance, Programs, Safety and Risk, Fundraising, and Chapter Development. Committees recommend action but do not replace full board authority unless delegated in writing. Committee chairs report at regular board meetings.

## Board Member Agreement

Each director agrees to attend meetings, prepare in advance, serve on at least one committee, make a personally meaningful annual contribution, support fundraising, protect confidential information, disclose conflicts, follow adopted policies, and speak with one voice after board decisions are made.

## Board Code of Conduct

Directors must act respectfully, avoid harassment or discrimination, protect the dignity of participants, never exploit the organization for personal gain, avoid unauthorized public statements, and immediately report suspected fraud, abuse, safety hazards, or policy violations.

## Conflict of Interest Policy

A conflict exists when personal, family, employment, financial, political, or organizational interests could influence judgment. Conflicts must be disclosed annually and whenever they arise. The Board determines whether a conflict exists and whether the conflicted person may participate in discussion. No conflicted person may vote on the matter.

## Confidentiality Agreement

Directors must protect participant information, donor records, personnel matters, medical information, legal matters, investigations, financial account details, and nonpublic strategy. Confidentiality continues after service ends. Unauthorized disclosure may result in removal.


## Board Review Items

The following standards are recommended for immediate use and must be reviewed, amended if needed, and formally adopted by the Board of Directors before legal reliance or public implementation:

* Final state registration requirements and charitable solicitation registrations.  
* Final insurance limits, carrier requirements, and rider eligibility rules.  
* Final background screening vendor and disqualifying criteria.  
* Final financial approval limits after the first annual budget is approved.  
* Final chapter territory map and national affiliation agreement.  

## Legal Notice

This manual is an organizational operating standard, not legal, tax, medical, or insurance advice. Lost Limb Riders should have qualified counsel, a certified public accountant, and a licensed insurance professional review all policies before adoption.
