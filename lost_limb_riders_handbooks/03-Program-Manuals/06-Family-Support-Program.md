# Lost Limb Riders Family Support Program Manual

## Purpose

The Family Support Initiative helps amputees, limb‑difference individuals, families, and caregivers find connection, practical support, peer encouragement, and trusted referrals. The program is not clinical treatment. It is human support backed by structure, boundaries, and safety.

## Eligibility

Participants may include individuals living with limb loss or limb difference, people facing possible amputation, family members, caregivers, and approved support persons. Services should be available regardless of motorcycle ownership. Priority may be given to individuals in active recovery, recent surgery, major adjustment, caregiver strain, or social isolation.

## Intake Process

The intake process should collect contact information, preferred communication method, emergency contact, relationship to limb loss, current support needs, accessibility needs, consent to receive support, confidentiality acknowledgment, referral source, and risk indicators. Intake staff must explain program limits and emergency procedures.

## Support Procedures

After intake, the Family Support Director or designee assigns the participant to appropriate support: peer mentor, family advocate, resource referral, group meeting, chapter connection, hospital visit if approved, or crisis resource referral. Contact notes must document date, type of contact, general topic, referrals made, and follow‑up plan without unnecessary private detail.

## Peer Mentor Guidelines

Peer mentors share lived experience, offer encouragement, model adaptation, and help participants feel less alone. Mentors must stay within boundaries, avoid medical advice, avoid promises, respect differences, maintain confidentiality, and escalate safety concerns. Mentors should receive supervision and periodic debriefing.

## Family Support Guidelines

Family Advocates support spouses, partners, parents, children, and caregivers as they adjust to new realities. Advocates may discuss communication, caregiver stress, home adjustment, community resources, and emotional strain. They may not provide therapy, legal advice, custody advice, or medical direction.

## Confidentiality Rules

Participant information is shared only with authorized program personnel and only for legitimate program purposes. Written consent is required before sharing stories, photos, testimonials, or identifiable information externally. Exceptions include imminent danger, suspected abuse or neglect, legal compulsion, or medical emergency.

## Referral Procedures

Lost Limb Riders should maintain a vetted referral list for emergency services, crisis lines, prosthetic providers, rehabilitation centers, mental health providers, veterans resources, transportation services, housing support, addiction recovery resources, and disability rights organizations. Referrals must be documented. Staff should avoid implying endorsement beyond available information unless a formal partnership exists.

## Crisis Response Procedures

If a participant expresses imminent intent to harm self or others, the volunteer or staff member must keep the person engaged if safe, contact emergency services, notify the Crisis Resource Coordinator, and document the incident. If danger is not imminent but concern is serious, escalate to the Crisis Resource Coordinator for same‑day follow‑up and referral. Volunteers must never promise secrecy in crisis situations.

## Hospital and Rehabilitation Partnerships

Hospital visits require facility approval, participant consent, trained representatives, infection control compliance, and professional boundaries. Lost Limb Riders representatives must follow facility rules and never interfere with medical care.
