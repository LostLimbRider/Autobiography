# Section 1: Program Overview

## 1.1 Mission

Lost Limb Riders connects amputees and their families with the community resources they need to live full, independent lives after limb loss. The Community Resource and Assistance Program puts that mission into action by assessing needs, providing trusted referrals, and offering limited direct assistance when available.

## 1.2 Purpose

This program exists to ensure that every person who reaches out to Lost Limb Riders receives a timely, organized response. We do not replace social service agencies, case managers, or government benefit programs. We bridge the gap between the person in need and the organizations that exist to help them.

We help people find what they need\u2014housing, transportation, food, prosthetics, mental health care, employment services, veteran benefits, and more\u2014without becoming a social service agency ourselves.

## 1.3 Scope

The Community Resource and Assistance Program covers:

- Receiving and screening requests for assistance
- Conducting needs assessments
- Providing referrals to trusted community resources
- Offering limited direct financial assistance when funding is available and criteria are met
- Maintaining an up-to-date Community Resource Directory
- Tracking referrals and following up on outcomes
- Documenting all assistance for program evaluation and accountability

## 1.4 Program Limitations

Lost Limb Riders is not a social service agency, a government benefits office, a medical provider, or a financial institution. This program does not:

- Provide ongoing case management or long-term support
- Guarantee approval of any assistance request
- Provide cash payments directly to individuals
- Replace existing benefits, insurance, or social services
- Offer legal advice, medical advice, or financial planning
- Transport individuals or serve as a medical transportation service
- Maintain open-ended files or indefinite assistance relationships

All program representatives must communicate these limitations clearly and early in every interaction.

## 1.5 Guiding Principles

**Meet people where they are.** Every person who contacts Lost Limb Riders is navigating something difficult. We respond with respect, patience, and honesty about what we can and cannot do.

**Do no harm.** A bad referral is worse than no referral. We vet our resources and maintain current information. We never send someone to a resource we have not confirmed.

**Stay in our lane.** We connect, we refer, we occasionally assist. We do not diagnose, treat, counsel, or case manage beyond resource navigation.

**Protect the organization.** Consistent documentation, clear financial controls, and appropriate boundaries protect Lost Limb Riders, its volunteers, and the people it serves.

**Preserve dignity.** No one asks for help because they want to. We treat every request with discretion and respect.

# Section 2: Eligibility

## 2.1 Who May Request Assistance

Any individual with limb loss or limb difference, or a family member or caregiver acting on their behalf, may request assistance through the Community Resource and Assistance Program.

Requests may also come from:

- Healthcare providers at partner hospitals and clinics
- Prosthetic and orthotic practitioners
- Social workers and discharge planners
- Physical and occupational therapists
- Veteran service officers
- Other nonprofit organizations

## 2.2 Priority Populations

When demand exceeds available resources, priority is given to:

- New amputees (within twelve months of amputation)
- Individuals without insurance or with significant coverage gaps
- Veterans
- Low-income individuals and families
- Individuals with no existing support network
- Caregivers of amputees

Priority is applied consistently and documented in each case. No preference is given based on race, religion, gender, sexual orientation, political affiliation, or Lost Limb Riders membership status.

## 2.3 Residency and Service-Area Requirements

Assistance is available to any individual who contacts Lost Limb Riders, regardless of geographic location. However:

- Direct financial assistance is typically limited to the organization\u2019s primary service area (defined by the Board of Directors and reviewed annually)
- Resource referrals are provided based on the individual\u2019s location, not Lost Limb Riders\u2019 location
- Out-of-area requests receive resource referrals but are unlikely to receive direct financial assistance

## 2.4 Frequency of Assistance

There is no hard limit on how many times an individual may request assistance. However:

- Each request is evaluated independently based on current need and available resources
- Repeated requests within a short time frame are reviewed for underlying issues that may require referral to a social service agency or case management program
- Financial assistance is limited to one approved request per individual per rolling twelve-month period unless exceptional circumstances are documented and approved by the Director of Medical Partnerships or Board designee

## 2.5 Exclusions

Lost Limb Riders does not provide assistance for:

- Cash payments directly to individuals (payments are made to vendors or service providers only)
- Medical procedures, surgeries, or hospital bills
- Prosthetic devices or prosthetic components (referrals only)
- Travel expenses outside the service area
- Legal fees or legal representation
- Ongoing subscription services or recurring bills
- Requests that appear fraudulent, duplicative, or based on misrepresentation

# Section 3: Intake Process

## 3.1 How Requests Are Received

Requests for assistance may arrive through any of the following channels:

- Phone call to the Lost Limb Riders contact number
- Email to the program\u2019s dedicated address
- Website contact form
- Referral from a healthcare partner
- Referral from a Lost Limb Riders chapter or volunteer
- In-person request at a Lost Limb Riders event or meeting

All requests are logged in the program\u2019s tracking system within one business day of receipt, regardless of whether they will ultimately be approved.

## 3.2 Emergency Screening

Upon receipt, every request is screened for urgency:

**Immediate emergency:** The individual reports a threat to life, safety, or health (no food, no shelter, active suicidal ideation, medical crisis). These requests are escalated immediately. The program representative connects the individual with appropriate emergency services\u2014dialing 911 if necessary, providing crisis hotline numbers, or connecting to emergency housing or food resources\u2014before any other action is taken.

**Urgent need:** The individual has a time-sensitive need that does not present an immediate danger (pending utility shut-off, transportation to a prosthetic appointment within 48 hours, imminent discharge from a facility without a safe destination). These requests receive priority handling with a goal of initial response within four business hours.

**Routine need:** The individual needs information or referrals but is not in crisis. These requests are processed in the order received with a goal of initial response within two business days.

## 3.3 Needs Assessment

Once urgency is determined, the program representative conducts a basic needs assessment:

- What is the specific need or request?
- Has the individual accessed other resources for this need?
- What is the timeline or deadline?
- What documentation is available or required?
- Is this a first-time request or a repeat request?

The needs assessment is documented in the tracking system. It does not require a formal in-person interview. It may be conducted by phone, email, or secure messaging.

## 3.4 Required Documentation

Documentation requirements depend on the type of assistance requested:

**Resource referrals:** No documentation required beyond the needs assessment.

**Direct financial assistance:** Documentation includes:

- Completed Financial Assistance Application
- Proof of the expense (bill, invoice, estimate, or notice)
- Government-issued identification
- Any additional documentation specified in Section 5 of this manual

The program representative explains all documentation requirements at the time of intake. Missing documentation is requested once. If not provided within fourteen calendar days, the request may be closed.

## 3.5 Intake Timeline

| Request Type | Initial Response Goal | Decision Timeline |
|--------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Immediate emergency | Immediate | N/A (refer to emergency services) |
| Urgent need | Within 4 business hours | Within 24 hours |
| Routine referral | Within 2 business days | Within 5 business days |
| Financial assistance | Within 2 business days | Within 10 business days |

If the program cannot meet these timelines due to volume or capacity, the individual is informed of the delay and given an expected response date.

# Section 4: Types of Assistance

## 4.1 Resource Referrals

Resource referrals are the primary form of assistance provided by this program. A resource referral is a direct connection to a vetted organization, agency, or provider that can address the individual\u2019s specific need.

Referrals include:

- Organization name and contact information
- Point of contact (if known)
- Description of services provided
- Eligibility requirements (if any)
- Application process or instructions
- Languages available
- Hours of operation

Referrals are provided in writing (email or printed handout) whenever possible. Verbal referrals are documented in the case file.

## 4.2 Transportation Assistance

Transportation assistance consists of referrals to transportation resources:

- Medical appointment transportation programs (Medicaid transport, VA transport, nonprofit medical ride programs)
- Public transit options and reduced-fare programs
- Ambulatory and wheelchair-accessible transportation services
- Volunteer driver programs (where available)
- Ride-share voucher programs (where available)

Lost Limb Riders does not operate its own transportation service. Volunteers do not transport individuals in personal vehicles under any circumstances.

## 4.3 Housing Referrals

Housing referrals connect individuals with:

- Emergency shelter programs
- Transitional housing programs
- Accessible housing resources
- Housing assistance agencies (rental assistance, security deposit assistance)
- Section 8 and public housing application assistance
- Disability-accessible housing locators

Lost Limb Riders does not provide housing, operate shelters, or pay rent directly.

## 4.4 Utility Assistance Referrals

Utility assistance referrals connect individuals with:

- Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program (LIHEAP)
- Local utility company assistance programs
- Community action agency energy assistance
- Crisis intervention programs for utility shut-off prevention

## 4.5 Food Resources

Food resource referrals include:

- SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program) application assistance
- Food bank locations and hours
- Meals on Wheels and home-delivered meal programs
- Community meal programs
- WIC (Women, Infants, and Children) referrals when applicable

## 4.6 Prosthetic Resources

Prosthetic resource assistance connects individuals with:

- Prosthetic and orthotic clinics that accept specific insurance or offer charity care
- Limb loss foundations that provide prosthetic funding or device grants
- Prosthetic reuse and recycling programs
- Insurance navigation assistance (referral to benefits counselors)
- Manufacturer patient assistance programs

Lost Limb Riders does not fund, purchase, or supply prosthetic devices.

## 4.7 Medical Equipment Referrals

Medical equipment referrals connect individuals with:

- Durable medical equipment (DME) providers
- Equipment loan closets and reuse programs
- Insurance coverage guidance for mobility aids
- Wheelchair and adaptive equipment resources
- Veterans Administration prosthetic and equipment services

## 4.8 Mental Health Referrals

Mental health referrals connect individuals with:

- Community mental health centers
- Sliding-scale therapy providers
- Support groups (amputee-specific and general)
- Crisis hotlines and warm lines
- Employee Assistance Programs (if applicable)
- Veteran mental health resources

Lost Limb Riders does not provide mental health counseling or therapy.

## 4.9 Employment Resources

Employment referrals connect individuals with:

- State vocational rehabilitation services
- Department of Veterans Affairs Vocational Rehabilitation and Employment (VR&E)
- Disability employment programs
- Job placement services for individuals with disabilities
- Telework and remote employment resources
- Social Security Ticket to Work program

## 4.10 Veteran Resources

Veteran resource referrals connect individuals with:

- VA benefits enrollment assistance
- VA prosthetic and sensory aids service
- VA caregiver support program
- Veterans service organizations (VSOs)
- Veterans Crisis Line
- Local VA medical center social work services

## 4.11 Disability Services

Disability service referrals connect individuals with:

- Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI) application assistance
- Supplemental Security Income (SSI) application assistance
- Disability advocacy organizations
- Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) information
- State protection and advocacy systems

## 4.12 Government Benefits Navigation

Benefits navigation referrals connect individuals with:

- State health insurance marketplace assistance
- Medicaid and Medicare enrollment resources
- SNAP application assistance
- Social Security benefits counseling
- State-specific disability benefit programs

# Section 5: Direct Financial Assistance (When Available)

## 5.1 Program Context

Lost Limb Riders may provide limited direct financial assistance when funding has been allocated by the Board of Directors. This is not an entitlement program. Funding availability varies based on donations, grants, and Board-approved budget allocations.

Financial assistance is not guaranteed, and no individual should be led to expect it.

## 5.2 Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for direct financial assistance, an individual must:

- Be an amputee or the primary caregiver of an amputee
- Have a documented, verifiable need
- Have no other available resource to address that need
- Be willing to provide the documentation required in Section 5.5
- Not have received financial assistance from Lost Limb Riders within the preceding twelve months (exceptional circumstances may be approved by the Director)

Eligibility is determined on a case-by-case basis. No one is automatically entitled to assistance.

## 5.3 Eligible Expenses

When funding is available, financial assistance may be used for:

- Emergency transportation to medical appointments (gas cards, bus passes, ride-share vouchers paid directly to provider)
- Temporary housing assistance (short-term hotel stay paid directly to hotel, security deposit assistance paid directly to landlord)
- Utility payments paid directly to the utility company (shut-off prevention only, not ongoing bills)
- Medical equipment not covered by insurance (paid directly to equipment provider)
- Prosthetic-related expenses not covered by insurance (paid directly to prosthetic clinic)
- Prescription medication assistance (paid directly to pharmacy)

## 5.4 Funding Limits

Maximum assistance per individual per rolling twelve-month period: $500 unless a higher amount is approved by the Director of Medical Partnerships or Board designee.

If multiple individuals from the same household request assistance, the household cap is $1,000 per rolling twelve-month period.

These limits may be suspended or modified by Board resolution during declared emergencies or disaster events.

## 5.5 Approval Authority

| Amount | Approval Authority |
|--------|-------------------|
| Up to $100 | Director of Medical Partnerships or designee |
| $101\u2013$250 | Director of Medical Partnerships |
| $251\u2013$500 | Director of Medical Partnerships + Board Treasurer notification |
| Over $500 | Board of Directors (or executive committee) approval required |

All approvals must be documented in writing before funds are disbursed.

## 5.6 Documentation Requirements

Financial assistance requires:

- Completed Financial Assistance Application form
- Copy of the bill, invoice, estimate, or notice showing the amount due
- Copy of government-issued identification for the individual receiving assistance
- Brief written statement describing why the need cannot be met through other resources
- Signed acknowledgment that this is a one-time assistance and does not guarantee future assistance

## 5.7 Payment Procedures

No cash payments are made to individuals. All payments are made directly to the vendor, provider, or agency.

Payment is made by organizational check or electronic payment. The check or payment is issued within ten business days of approval.

The payment is documented in the financial records with:

- Date of approval
- Approver name and title
- Payee name
- Amount
- Purpose
- Case reference number

## 5.8 Fraud Prevention

Lost Limb Riders takes reasonable steps to prevent fraud:

- Payments are made to vendors, never to individuals
- Duplicate requests are checked against the program database
- Requests that appear inconsistent or suspicious are reviewed by the Director before action
- False or fraudulent information on an application may result in permanent ineligibility
- Suspected fraud may be reported to appropriate authorities

# Section 6: Community Resource Directory

## 6.1 Purpose

The Community Resource Directory is the central reference tool of this program. It contains vetted organizations, agencies, and providers organized by service type. Every referral made through this program comes from the Directory.

## 6.2 Directory Maintenance

The Directory is maintained by the Director of Medical Partnerships or a designee.

Each entry includes:

- Organization name
- Service type
- Contact information (phone, email, website)
- Physical address
- Service area
- Eligibility requirements
- Languages available
- Hours of operation
- Application process
- Last verified date

## 6.3 Verification Schedule

Every entry in the Directory is verified at least once per calendar year.

Verification consists of:

- Confirming the organization still provides the listed service
- Confirming contact information is current
- Confirming eligibility requirements have not changed
- Checking for any complaints or concerns about the organization\u2019s services

Verification results are documented, and the Directory is updated within five business days of any change.

## 6.4 Directory Categories

The Directory is organized into the following categories:

- Hospitals and trauma centers
- Prosthetic and orthotic clinics
- Rehabilitation centers
- Housing and shelter agencies
- Transportation providers
- Food banks and meal programs
- Utility assistance programs
- Mental health providers
- Legal aid organizations
- Employment and vocational services
- Veteran services
- Disability services
- Government benefits offices
- Adaptive recreation and sports organizations
- Support groups and peer support organizations

## 6.5 Confidential Referrals

When a referral is made, the individual\u2019s name is not shared with the resource organization unless the individual has given explicit consent. Referrals are provided to the individual, who then contacts the resource directly.

# Section 7: Case Management Standards

## 7.1 Scope of Case Activity

This program does not provide clinical case management, ongoing case coordination, or long-term follow-up. Case activity is limited to:

- Intake and needs assessment
- Referral provision
- Follow-up to verify the referral was received and whether it was helpful
- Case closure

## 7.2 Follow-Up Expectations

Follow-up is conducted for every assistance request:

**Resource referrals:** The individual is contacted within fourteen days to ask whether they connected with the resource and whether the resource was helpful.

**Financial assistance:** The individual is contacted within thirty days to confirm the need was met and to assess whether additional referrals are needed.

**No answer:** If the individual does not respond to two follow-up attempts (one phone call and one email or text message), the case is closed.

## 7.3 Case Documentation

Every request for assistance is documented in the program tracking system. Documentation includes:

- Date of request
- Channel (phone, email, referral, etc.)
- Individual\u2019s name and contact information
- Type of need
- Urgency screening result
- Assistance provided (referrals given, financial assistance details)
- Follow-up results

## 7.4 Referral Tracking

Each referral provided is logged with:

- Resource organization name
- Date provided
- Method of delivery (verbal, email, printed)
- Follow-up result (connected or not connected, helpful or not helpful)

## 7.5 Case Closure

A case is closed when:

- The need has been addressed (assistance provided, referral made, connection confirmed)
- The individual has declined assistance
- The individual cannot be reached after two follow-up attempts
- The request is outside the program\u2019s scope and no appropriate referral exists
- Sixty days have passed since the last meaningful contact

Closed cases are retained in the program database in accordance with the record retention policy in Section 8.

# Section 8: Confidentiality

## 8.1 Privacy Standards

All information shared by individuals requesting assistance is confidential. It is used only for program purposes and is not shared outside Lost Limb Riders without the individual\u2019s written consent, except as required by law.

Program representatives do not discuss individual cases with anyone outside the organization unless consent has been given or disclosure is legally required.

## 8.2 Record Retention

Program records are retained for three years from the date of case closure.

After three years, records are securely destroyed by shredding (paper) or permanent deletion (electronic).

Records that are part of a legal proceeding, audit, or pending investigation are retained until the proceeding is resolved.

## 8.3 Information Sharing

Lost Limb Riders may share case information internally for program operations and quality improvement purposes. Internal access is limited to individuals who need the information to perform their program duties.

External information sharing occurs only:

- With the individual\u2019s written consent
- As required by mandatory reporting laws
- In response to a valid subpoena or court order

## 8.4 Participant Consent

Before any information about an individual is shared with a third party (including resource organizations), the individual must give written or recorded verbal consent specifying:

- What information may be shared
- With whom it may be shared
- The purpose of sharing
- How long the consent is valid

Consent is documented in the case file. Individuals may revoke consent at any time.

# Section 9: Risk Management

## 9.1 Financial Accountability

All financial assistance transactions are recorded and reconciled monthly. The program maintains a separate ledger for financial assistance funds, distinct from general operating funds.

Monthly reconciliation is reviewed by the Director of Medical Partnerships or Board Treasurer.

Annual financial reports include a summary of financial assistance distributed, including total funds disbursed, number of individuals served, and average assistance amount.

## 9.2 Emergency Situations

If a program representative encounters a situation involving:

- Immediate threat of harm (suicide, violence, abuse)
- Medical emergency
- Observation of illegal activity

The representative\u2019s first priority is safety. Call 911 if appropriate. Notify the individual\u2019s emergency contact if known and appropriate. Document the situation and notify the Director within twenty-four hours.

## 9.3 Mandatory Reporting

Program representatives are mandatory reporters of child abuse, elder abuse, and dependent adult abuse in all states where Lost Limb Riders operates. Representatives must:

- Know the reporting requirements in their state
- Report suspected abuse immediately to the appropriate agency
- Document the report in the case file (what was reported, to whom, and when)
- Notify the Director within twenty-four hours

## 9.4 Volunteer Safety

Volunteers serving in this program do not:

- Visit individuals in their homes alone
- Transport individuals in personal vehicles
- Provide financial assistance from personal funds
- Share personal contact information with individuals being served
- Meet individuals in private or unsecured locations

Volunteers who feel unsafe at any time should end the interaction, move to a public or secure location, and notify the Director.

## 9.5 Conflict-of-Interest Safeguards

No program representative may:

- Approve financial assistance for themselves or a family member
- Refer an individual to a business in which they have a financial interest
- Accept gifts, payments, or favors from individuals seeking assistance
- Use program resources for personal benefit or the benefit of friends or family

Any actual or potential conflict of interest must be disclosed to the Director immediately.

# Section 10: Program Metrics

## 10.1 Metrics Collected

The following metrics are tracked and reported quarterly:

- Number of assistance requests received
- Number of resource referrals provided
- Number of referrals completed (individual confirmed connection with resource)
- Number of individuals receiving direct financial assistance
- Total financial assistance funds distributed
- Average financial assistance amount per individual
- Follow-up completion rate (percentage of cases with completed follow-up within required timeline)
- Participant satisfaction score (average rating from follow-up surveys)
- Most common types of assistance requested
- Number of requests outside program scope

## 10.2 Reporting

Metrics are compiled quarterly and reviewed by the Director of Medical Partnerships and Board of Directors.

An annual program summary is prepared for Board review and may be included in grant reports, donor communications, and public transparency reporting.

## 10.3 Participant Satisfaction

Participants are asked at the time of follow-up:

- Did the referral or assistance meet your need?
- Was the information provided clear and helpful?
- How satisfied are you with your interaction with Lost Limb Riders? (1\u20135 scale)
- Is there anything we could have done better?

Responses are anonymous. Results are aggregated and reported quarterly.

## 10.4 Continuous Improvement

If any metric shows a declining trend for two consecutive quarters, the Director initiates a review to identify causes and develop corrective actions.

# Section 11: Standard Operating Procedures

## SOP 1: Receiving a Request for Assistance

**Purpose:** To ensure every request is captured, logged, and screened consistently.

**Scope:** All program representatives.

**Prerequisites:** Access to the program tracking system.

**Steps:**

1. Answer the incoming request by phone, email, or in person within the response timelines in Section 3.5.
2. Collect the individual\u2019s name, contact information, and brief description of the need.
3. Determine urgency per Section 3.2.
4. If immediate emergency: stop all other activity. Provide emergency resources or call 911. Document the interaction. Notify the Director within 24 hours.
5. If urgent or routine: log the request in the tracking system with date, channel, and basic information.
6. Inform the individual about the program\u2019s limitations and what they can expect next.
7. Assign a case reference number.

**Documentation Required:** Case record with date, channel, individual information, and urgency determination.

**Completion Criteria:** Request is logged with a case reference number. Individual has been told what to expect next.

---

## SOP 2: Conducting a Needs Assessment

**Purpose:** To understand the full scope of the individual\u2019s need so appropriate resources can be identified.

**Scope:** All requests except immediate emergencies (which are handled first, with assessment later if appropriate).

**Prerequisites:** Case reference number assigned.

**Steps:**

1. Contact the individual by their preferred method within the response timeline.
2. Use the Needs Assessment form to guide the conversation.
3. Ask about the specific need, timeline, existing resources accessed, and barriers.
4. Determine whether the request falls within program scope.
5. If outside scope: explain politely, document the reason, close the case.
6. If within scope: identify potential resources from the Community Resource Directory.
7. Record the assessment in the case record.

**Documentation Required:** Completed Needs Assessment form or equivalent documentation in the case record.

**Completion Criteria:** Need is fully understood. Appropriate resources have been identified. Individual is informed of next steps.

---

## SOP 3: Making a Referral

**Purpose:** To connect individuals with vetted community resources.

**Scope:** All program representatives making referrals.

**Prerequisites:** Completed needs assessment. Resource identified in the Community Resource Directory.

**Steps:**

1. Select the appropriate resource from the Directory.
2. Verify the resource is still active and the contact information is current. If not verified within the past six months, confirm by phone or web before referring.
3. Provide the referral to the individual in writing (email or printed handout) whenever possible.
4. Explain what the resource provides, any eligibility requirements, and how to apply.
5. Confirm the individual understands the referral and has no additional questions.
6. Log the referral in the case record (resource name, date, method provided).
7. Schedule a follow-up in fourteen days.

**Documentation Required:** Referral logged in case record.

**Completion Criteria:** Referral has been provided and logged. Follow-up is scheduled.

---

## SOP 4: Approving Direct Financial Assistance

**Purpose:** To ensure financial assistance is approved consistently, with appropriate documentation and authorization.

**Scope:** Director of Medical Partnerships and authorized designees.

**Prerequisites:** Completed Financial Assistance Application. Documentation as specified in Section 5.5. Verification that the individual has not exceeded the twelve-month limit.

**Steps:**

1. Review the Financial Assistance Application and supporting documentation for completeness.
2. Verify the request meets the eligibility criteria in Section 5.2.
3. Confirm the expense is an eligible expense per Section 5.3.
4. Determine the approval authority level per Section 5.4.
5. Obtain required approvals in writing.
6. If denied: notify the individual in writing with the reason. Offer resource referrals as appropriate. Close the case.
7. If approved: notify the individual. Arrange payment per Section 5.6.
8. Log the approval and payment in the case record and financial ledger.

**Documentation Required:** Financial Assistance Application, supporting documentation, written approval, payment record.

**Completion Criteria:** Payment has been issued or denial has been communicated.

---

## SOP 5: Following Up

**Purpose:** To confirm whether the individual connected with the resource and whether their need was met.

**Scope:** All cases where a referral was provided.

**Prerequisites:** Fourteen days have passed since the referral was provided (or as specified in Section 7.2).

**Steps:**

1. Contact the individual by their preferred method.
2. Ask whether they contacted the resource.
3. If yes: ask whether the resource was helpful and whether the need was met. Record the outcome.
4. If no: ask whether they still need assistance. If yes, identify barriers and offer a new referral or additional support. If no, confirm and close.
5. Ask the satisfaction survey questions from Section 10.3.
6. If the individual does not respond, make one additional attempt (different method if possible). If no response after two attempts, close the case.
7. Log the follow-up in the case record.

**Documentation Required:** Follow-up log entry in case record.

**Completion Criteria:** Follow-up is complete, or case is closed after no response to two attempts.

---

## SOP 6: Closing a Case

**Purpose:** To ensure cases are closed consistently with complete documentation.

**Scope:** All cases.

**Prerequisites:** One of the closure conditions from Section 7.5 is met.

**Steps:**

1. Review the case record for completeness.
2. Ensure all referrals, follow-ups, and financial transactions are documented.
3. Note the closure reason in the case record.
4. Mark the case as closed in the tracking system.
5. If financial assistance was provided, ensure the financial record is reconciled.

**Documentation Required:** Case record with closure reason and date.

**Completion Criteria:** Case is closed in the tracking system.

Anonymous survey collecting feedback on the assistance received, quality of interaction, and suggestions for improvement.
