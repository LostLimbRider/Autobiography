# Lost Limb Riders Corporate Governance Manual

## Purpose

Lost Limb Riders exists to support amputees, limb‑different individuals, and their families through community, mentorship, public awareness, adaptive opportunities, and practical resources—including housing and independence advocacy—that restore hope, rebuild confidence, and remind every person that nobody is left behind and nobody stands alone. This manual establishes the governing system needed for a professional nonprofit capable of local operation, hospital partnership, responsible fundraising, and national expansion.

## Chain of Command

Board of Directors  
Executive Leadership  
Department Directors  
Program Coordinators  
Volunteers and Members

The Board governs mission, fiduciary duty, strategic direction, legal compliance, and executive accountability. Executive Leadership manages daily operations. Department Directors manage assigned operating areas. Program Coordinators execute approved programs. Volunteers and members serve within assigned roles, policies, and training requirements.

## Board Structure

The Board should include five to eleven voting directors. The recommended launch structure is seven voting directors: Chairperson, Vice Chairperson, Secretary, Treasurer, and three At‑Large Directors. A larger national organization may expand to eleven directors by board vote.

At least one director should have lived experience with limb loss or limb difference. At least one director should have finance, accounting, or nonprofit fiscal experience. At least one director should have healthcare, rehabilitation, veterans, disability services, safety, or risk management experience.

## Board Member Requirements

Directors must support the mission, act in the organization’s best interest, maintain confidentiality, disclose conflicts, attend meetings, participate in committees, review materials before votes, and comply with all adopted policies. Directors must be at least eighteen years old and able to exercise independent judgment.

## Officer Responsibilities

### Chairperson

The Chairperson leads board meetings, sets agendas with Executive Leadership, ensures committees function, represents the Board when authorized, conducts annual executive performance review coordination, and protects the integrity of governance. The Chairperson does not manage daily operations unless also formally serving in an executive role approved by the Board.

### Vice Chairperson

The Vice Chairperson supports the Chairperson, performs Chairperson duties when the Chairperson is unavailable, leads special governance projects, and assists with board recruitment and accountability.

### Secretary

The Secretary maintains official minutes, board resolutions, corporate records, director rosters, meeting notices, attendance records, and adopted policy archives. Minutes must record motions, seconds, votes, recusals, abstentions, and action items.

### Treasurer

The Treasurer oversees financial governance, reviews monthly financial reports, chairs or supports the Finance Committee, confirms internal controls are followed, and presents financial information to the Board. The Treasurer does not serve as the sole person with custody, approval, and reconciliation authority over funds.

### At‑Large Directors

At‑Large Directors provide independent oversight, committee leadership, community representation, fundraising support, and strategic guidance.

## Election Process and Terms

Directors are elected by majority vote of seated directors unless bylaws later provide member voting rights. Initial directors should serve staggered terms so continuity is protected. Recommended terms are two years, renewable for up to three consecutive terms. After six consecutive years, a director should rotate off for at least one year unless the Board approves an exception for continuity.

Officer elections should occur annually at the first regular meeting of the fiscal year. Vacancies may be filled by majority vote for the remainder of the term.

## Voting Procedures

A quorum is a majority of current voting directors. Each director has one vote. Routine motions pass by majority vote of directors present once quorum is established. Major actions require two‑thirds approval of current directors: bylaw amendments, executive hiring or removal, annual budget approval, merger, dissolution, chapter revocation, real estate obligations, loans, and commitments over board‑approved thresholds.

Directors with conflicts must disclose the conflict, leave discussion when directed, and abstain from voting. The minutes must document the disclosure and abstention.

## Meeting Procedures

The Board should meet at least quarterly. Monthly meetings are recommended during the launch phase. Agendas should be distributed at least five calendar days before regular meetings. Emergency meetings may be called by the Chairperson or any three directors with reasonable notice.

Meetings should include mission moment, approval of prior minutes, financial report, executive report, committee reports, old business, new business, action items, and executive session when needed.

## Committee Structure

Standing committees are established by the Corporate Bylaws, Article VI, Section 6.01, and include: Executive, Finance, Governance, Audit, Fundraising, Programs, Safety and Risk, and Membership. Chapter Development may be established as a temporary committee or task force per Bylaws Article VI, Section 6.02. Committees recommend action but do not replace full board authority unless delegated in writing by Board resolution. Committee chairs report at regular board meetings. Full committee charters are maintained in the Governance Framework (`GOVERNANCE-FRAMEWORK/06-Committee-Charters.md`).

## Board Member Agreement

Each director agrees to attend meetings, prepare in advance, serve on at least one committee, make a personally meaningful annual contribution, support fundraising, protect confidential information, disclose conflicts, follow adopted policies, and speak with one voice after board decisions are made.

## Board Code of Conduct

Directors must act respectfully, avoid harassment or discrimination, protect the dignity of participants, never exploit the organization for personal gain, avoid unauthorized public statements, and immediately report suspected fraud, abuse, safety hazards, or policy violations.

## Conflict of Interest Policy

A conflict exists when personal, family, employment, financial, political, or organizational interests could influence judgment. Conflicts must be disclosed annually and whenever they arise. The Board determines whether a conflict exists and whether the conflicted person may participate in discussion. No conflicted person may vote on the matter.

## Confidentiality Agreement

Directors must protect participant information, donor records, personnel matters, medical information, legal matters, investigations, financial account details, and nonpublic strategy. Confidentiality continues after service ends. Unauthorized disclosure may result in removal.


## Board Review Items

The following standards are recommended for immediate use and must be reviewed, amended if needed, and formally adopted by the Board of Directors before legal reliance or public implementation:

* Final state registration requirements and charitable solicitation registrations.  
* Final insurance limits, carrier requirements, and rider eligibility rules.  
* Final background screening vendor and disqualifying criteria.  
* Final financial approval limits after the first annual budget is approved.  
* Final chapter territory map and national affiliation agreement.

# Lost Limb Riders Administrative Operations Manual

## Purpose

This manual defines the administrative operating system for Lost Limb Riders. It creates clear authority, consistent records, responsible communication, and scalable procedures for local and national operations.

## Executive Leadership Authority

### Founder / CEO

Purpose: Protect the founding vision, public mission, culture, and long‑term direction of Lost Limb Riders.

Responsibilities include mission advocacy, major relationship development, public speaking, board collaboration, strategic planning, and culture protection. The Founder / CEO may approve public mission messaging, represent the organization when authorized, and recommend strategic partnerships. Operational authority must be defined by board resolution and employment or volunteer agreement.

Reports to: Board of Directors.

Success measurements: mission consistency, partnership growth, donor confidence, chapter culture, public credibility, and adherence to board‑approved strategy.

### Executive Director

Purpose: Lead day‑to‑day operations and convert board strategy into action.

Responsibilities include department supervision, annual operating plan development, staff and volunteer leadership, budget implementation, compliance tracking, program oversight, board reporting, partnership execution, and performance management.

Decision authority includes routine operating decisions within approved budget and policy, hiring recommendations, department assignments, vendor recommendations, and emergency operational actions.

Reports to: Board of Directors.

Success measurements: program delivery, compliance, budget adherence, volunteer retention, partnership satisfaction, safety performance, and timely reporting.

### Deputy Executive Director

Purpose: Support executive operations and maintain continuity when the Executive Director is unavailable.

Responsibilities include cross‑department coordination, special projects, chapter support, administrative supervision, policy implementation, and operational troubleshooting.

Reports to: Executive Director.

Success measurements: project completion, department responsiveness, record accuracy, chapter support, and continuity of operations.

## Department Structure

### Administration Department

The Administration Department manages records, calendars, official correspondence, document control, meeting logistics, compliance calendars, and administrative support. Required positions are Director of Administration, Administrative Assistant, and Records Compliance Officer.

### Membership Department

The Membership Department manages member applications, member records, renewals, onboarding, chapter coordination, member communication, and member engagement. Required positions are Membership Director, Membership Coordinator, and Chapter Directors.

### Family Support Initiative Department

The Family Support Initiative Department manages intake, peer support, family advocacy, resource navigation, referrals, crisis escalation, and support documentation. Required positions are Family Support Director, Peer Support Coordinator, Family Advocate, and Crisis Resource Coordinator.

### Outreach Department

The Outreach Department builds relationships with hospitals, rehabilitation centers, prosthetic clinics, veterans organizations, disability organizations, civic groups, and community partners. Required positions are Outreach Director, Hospital Liaison, and Community Relations Coordinator.

### Motorcycle Operations Department

The Motorcycle Operations Department manages ride planning, rider safety, route review, ride briefings, road guard coordination, motorcycle eligibility, and post‑ride reporting. Required positions are Riding Operations Director, Ride Captain, Safety Officer, and Road Guard Team.

### Events Department

The Events Department manages public events, fundraisers, community gatherings, volunteer scheduling, site logistics, permits, and event evaluation. Required positions are Events Director, Event Coordinator, and Volunteer Coordinator.

### Fundraising Department

The Fundraising Department manages donor cultivation, grants, sponsorships, campaigns, donor records, recognition, and fundraising compliance. Required positions are Fundraising Director, Grant Writer, Sponsorship Coordinator, and Donor Relations Manager.

### Finance Department

The Finance Department manages bookkeeping, accounts payable, deposits, purchasing, reports, reconciliations, budgets, and financial documentation. Required positions are Finance Director, Bookkeeper, and Purchasing Officer.

### Marketing Department

The Marketing Department manages brand identity, public communications, website content, media coordination, social media, newsletters, and storytelling standards. Required positions are Communications Director, Social Media Manager, Website Administrator, and Media Coordinator.

### Legal and Risk Management

Legal and Risk Management supports contract review, insurance coordination, risk assessment, safety policy, incident review, legal compliance, and liability reduction. Required positions are Legal Advisor, Insurance/Risk Manager, and Safety Coordinator.

## Administrative Records

Official records must be stored in a secure digital repository with access controls. Required records include board minutes, bylaws, policies, insurance documents, waivers, participant records, volunteer files, donor records, financial reports, contracts, chapter agreements, incident reports, and compliance filings.

## Document Control

Each policy must include title, version date, approving authority, owner, review cycle, and replacement history. Policies should be reviewed annually. Emergency policy changes may be approved by Executive Leadership and ratified at the next board meeting.

## Communication Procedures

Official external communications must be accurate, respectful, mission‑aligned, and approved by the proper department. Sensitive matters involving participants, incidents, legal issues, financial matters, or personnel must be escalated to Executive Leadership before response.

## Performance Expectations

Every leadership role must maintain records, meet deadlines, follow budget limits, report problems early, treat participants with dignity, and support a culture of accountability without ego.

# Lost Limb Riders Volunteer Handbook

## Welcome

Volunteers are the hands, boots, wheels, and heart of Lost Limb Riders. The work is personal. People come to this organization after loss, fear, surgery, pain, addiction, grief, or a hard reset in life. Volunteers must bring compassion, reliability, humility, and common sense.

## Volunteer Application

Applicants must provide full legal name, contact information, emergency contact, availability, areas of interest, relevant experience, motorcycle endorsement if applying for ride roles, references, consent for background screening when required, and agreement to follow all policies.

## Volunteer Agreement

Volunteers agree to serve within assigned duties, follow safety rules, protect confidential information, avoid discrimination and harassment, report concerns promptly, represent the organization professionally, complete required training, and accept supervision. Volunteer service may be ended by either party at any time.

## Code of Conduct

Volunteers must treat every participant, family member, donor, partner, and fellow volunteer with dignity. Volunteers may not use their role to sell products, push religion or politics, seek romantic or financial advantage, disclose private stories without consent, arrive impaired, carry weapons where prohibited, or ignore safety instructions.

## Training Requirements

All volunteers must complete orientation, mission and culture training, confidentiality training, boundaries training, reporting procedures, and role‑specific training. Ride volunteers must complete ride safety orientation. Family support volunteers must complete trauma‑informed support, crisis escalation, and referral boundary training. Event volunteers must complete site safety and public interaction training.

## Background Check Policy

Background checks are required for volunteers working with vulnerable participants, minors, medical partner settings, financial handling, transportation, overnight events, or family support. A prior record does not automatically disqualify an applicant. Decisions should consider role relevance, time elapsed, rehabilitation, honesty in disclosure, and participant safety. Final criteria must be approved by the Board.

## Volunteer Roles

Volunteer roles may include event support, ride support, peer support, hospital outreach assistance, administrative support, fundraising support, marketing support, chapter support, and logistics support. Volunteers may only perform duties they are trained and approved to perform.

## Boundaries

Volunteers are not therapists, doctors, lawyers, financial advisors, or emergency responders unless separately licensed and assigned in that capacity. Volunteers may listen, encourage, share lived experience when appropriate, and connect people with resources. They must not diagnose, prescribe, promise outcomes, provide private transportation without approval, or manage crises alone.

## Recognition Program

Lost Limb Riders should recognize volunteers through thank‑you notes, service pins, annual appreciation events, spotlight stories with consent, milestone certificates, leadership opportunities, and board recognition. Recognition should honor reliability, safety, compassion, and mission impact, not just hours served.

## Corrective Action

Concerns may result in coaching, retraining, reassignment, suspension, or dismissal. Serious misconduct, safety violations, harassment, confidentiality breaches, theft, impairment, or abuse may result in immediate removal.

# Lost Limb Riders Family Support Program Manual

## Purpose

The Family Support Initiative helps amputees, limb‑difference individuals, families, and caregivers find connection, practical support, peer encouragement, and trusted referrals. The program is not clinical treatment. It is human support backed by structure, boundaries, and safety.

## Eligibility

Participants may include individuals living with limb loss or limb difference, people facing possible amputation, family members, caregivers, and approved support persons. Services should be available regardless of motorcycle ownership. Priority may be given to individuals in active recovery, recent surgery, major adjustment, caregiver strain, or social isolation.

## Intake Process

The intake process should collect contact information, preferred communication method, emergency contact, relationship to limb loss, current support needs, accessibility needs, consent to receive support, confidentiality acknowledgment, referral source, and risk indicators. Intake staff must explain program limits and emergency procedures.

## Support Procedures

After intake, the Family Support Director or designee assigns the participant to appropriate support: peer mentor, family advocate, resource referral, group meeting, chapter connection, hospital visit if approved, or crisis resource referral. Contact notes must document date, type of contact, general topic, referrals made, and follow‑up plan without unnecessary private detail.

## Peer Mentor Guidelines

Peer mentors share lived experience, offer encouragement, model adaptation, and help participants feel less alone. Mentors must stay within boundaries, avoid medical advice, avoid promises, respect differences, maintain confidentiality, and escalate safety concerns. Mentors should receive supervision and periodic debriefing.

## Family Support Guidelines

Family Advocates support spouses, partners, parents, children, and caregivers as they adjust to new realities. Advocates may discuss communication, caregiver stress, home adjustment, community resources, and emotional strain. They may not provide therapy, legal advice, custody advice, or medical direction.

## Confidentiality Rules

Participant information is shared only with authorized program personnel and only for legitimate program purposes. Written consent is required before sharing stories, photos, testimonials, or identifiable information externally. Exceptions include imminent danger, suspected abuse or neglect, legal compulsion, or medical emergency.

## Referral Procedures

Lost Limb Riders should maintain a vetted referral list for emergency services, crisis lines, prosthetic providers, rehabilitation centers, mental health providers, veterans resources, transportation services, housing support, addiction recovery resources, and disability rights organizations. Referrals must be documented. Staff should avoid implying endorsement beyond available information unless a formal partnership exists.

## Crisis Response Procedures

If a participant expresses imminent intent to harm self or others, the volunteer or staff member must keep the person engaged if safe, contact emergency services, notify the Crisis Resource Coordinator, and document the incident. If danger is not imminent but concern is serious, escalate to the Crisis Resource Coordinator for same‑day follow‑up and referral. Volunteers must never promise secrecy in crisis situations.

## Hospital and Rehabilitation Partnerships

Hospital visits require facility approval, participant consent, trained representatives, infection control compliance, and professional boundaries. Lost Limb Riders representatives must follow facility rules and never interfere with medical care.

# Lost Limb Riders Safety and Risk Management Manual

## Purpose

Safety is not paperwork. Safety is how Lost Limb Riders protects people who already know what it means for life to change in one second. This manual establishes waivers, risk controls, ride standards, event procedures, emergency response, and incident reporting.

## Risk Management Policy

Every program must identify foreseeable risks, assign responsible leaders, use written procedures, confirm training, document incidents, and improve after review. High‑risk activities require Executive Leadership approval and insurance review.

## Participant Waiver

Participants must acknowledge voluntary participation, assumption of ordinary risks, medical responsibility, emergency contact permission, media consent choice, confidentiality expectations, and release language reviewed by counsel. Separate consent is required for minors or dependent adults.

## Volunteer Waiver

Volunteers must acknowledge voluntary service, role limits, physical demands, safety compliance, personal insurance obligations when using personal vehicles, emergency contact permission, and release language reviewed by counsel.

## Ride Waiver

Ride participants must acknowledge motorcycle risks, helmet and legal compliance, licensing requirements, insurance responsibility, sobriety requirement, mechanical responsibility, route compliance, ride captain authority, emergency procedures, and weather cancellation authority.

## Ride Safety Procedures

Every organized ride must have a Ride Captain, Safety Officer, route plan, emergency contact list, pre‑ride briefing, weather review, fuel plan, rest stops, sweep rider when possible, and post‑ride check‑in. Riders must have valid license or endorsement as required by law, legal registration, insurance, and a roadworthy motorcycle.

## Event Safety Procedures

Every event must have a site lead, emergency plan, accessible layout, first aid location, incident report forms, volunteer briefing, weather plan for outdoor events, crowd control plan when needed, and clear alcohol policy. Events involving vendors, food, raffles, minors, or public roads require additional compliance review.

## Emergency Response Plan

In an emergency, call emergency services first. Protect the scene if safe, provide aid only within training, notify the event or ride leader, secure participant information, contact emergency contacts when appropriate, preserve facts, and complete an incident report within twenty‑four hours. Serious incidents must be reported to Executive Leadership immediately.

## Incident Reporting System

Reportable incidents include injury, crash, medical emergency, threat, harassment, abuse allegation, property damage, theft, missing person, intoxication, confidentiality breach, near miss, partner complaint, and policy violation. Reports must include date, time, location, people involved, witnesses, description, actions taken, emergency services, photos if appropriate, and follow‑up recommendations.

## Insurance Review

The Insurance/Risk Manager must maintain general liability, directors and officers coverage, volunteer coverage if available, event coverage, cyber coverage if needed, and any ride‑specific coverage recommended by the broker. Insurance certificates must be tracked for venues, partners, vendors, and chapters.

# Lost Limb Riders Financial Procedures Manual

## Purpose

This manual protects donor trust, board accountability, grant eligibility, and the long‑term survival of Lost Limb Riders. Money must be handled clean, documented, and boring. Boring is good. Boring keeps the doors open.

## Budget Procedures

The Executive Director and Finance Director prepare an annual budget before each fiscal year. Department Directors submit budget requests with program goals, expected revenue, expenses, staffing needs, and assumptions. The Finance Committee reviews the draft and recommends approval to the Board. The Board approves the final budget before implementation.

## Expense Approval Process

Routine expenses must be within approved budget and supported by receipts or invoices. Recommended launch approval limits are: Department Director up to $250, Executive Director up to $1,000, Finance Committee review above $1,000, and Board approval above $5,000 or for any unbudgeted long‑term obligation. These limits are recommended standards for board review.

## Segregation of Duties

No single person should authorize, spend, receive, record, and reconcile funds. Deposits should be logged by one person and reconciled by another. Bank statements should be reviewed monthly by the Treasurer or designee who does not perform bookkeeping.

## Donation Handling Procedures

Donations must be recorded promptly with donor name, amount, date, restriction if any, payment method, campaign, and acknowledgment status. Cash donations require two‑person counting and signed deposit logs. Restricted donations must be used only for the stated purpose or returned with donor agreement if the purpose cannot be fulfilled.

## Grant Tracking System

Each grant must have a file containing application, award letter, budget, restrictions, reporting deadlines, deliverables, reimbursement rules, contacts, and final report. The Grant Writer and Finance Director must reconcile grant spending monthly.

## Sponsorship Management

Sponsorship agreements must define sponsor benefits, payment schedule, recognition, logo use, exclusivity if any, event expectations, cancellation terms, and values alignment. Sponsors may not control participant services, program eligibility, safety decisions, or clinical referrals.

## Purchasing Procedures

Purchases must serve mission needs, fit approved budget, use reasonable pricing, and avoid conflicts of interest. Purchases above board‑approved thresholds should include multiple quotes unless impractical. Personal reimbursement requires receipts and approval by someone other than the purchaser.

## Financial Reporting

Monthly reports should include statement of activity, statement of financial position, budget‑to‑actual report, cash balance, restricted funds report, major payables, grant status, and fundraising pipeline. The Board receives financial reports at every regular meeting.

## Audit and Review

The Board should approve an annual financial review process. When revenue reaches a board‑defined threshold or grant requirements demand it, Lost Limb Riders should obtain an independent review or audit.

# Lost Limb Riders Chapter Development Manual

## Purpose

Chapters allow Lost Limb Riders to grow without losing its soul. This manual creates standards for local groups operating under the national mission, name, safety expectations, and accountability system.

## Chapter Requirements

A chapter must have at least five active members, one approved Chapter Director, one safety lead for ride activity, one administrative contact, proof of training completion, signed affiliation agreement, approved territory, and ability to report activity monthly. Chapters must follow national brand standards, safety standards, confidentiality rules, and financial procedures.

## Chapter Approval Process

Interested organizers submit a chapter interest form, leadership roster, service area, community need statement, proposed activities, partner opportunities, and safety plan. National leadership interviews organizers, reviews risk, verifies alignment, and recommends approval or denial. The Board or delegated Chapter Development Committee grants provisional approval.

## Provisional Chapter Period

New chapters should operate provisionally for six to twelve months. During this period, they must complete training, submit monthly reports, host at least one approved community activity, follow financial rules, and demonstrate safe culture. Full approval requires successful review.

## Chapter Leadership Structure

Each chapter should include Chapter Director, Assistant Chapter Director, Chapter Secretary, Chapter Safety Lead, Membership Lead, Events Lead, and Family Support Liaison when trained. Smaller chapters may combine duties with national approval, but safety and finance duties must remain separated where money is handled.

## Chapter Reporting Requirements

Monthly reports must include membership updates, volunteer hours, events held, rides held, incidents or near misses, participant support contacts, partner contacts, funds raised, expenses, needs, and upcoming activities. Serious incidents must be reported immediately.

## National Organization Standards

Chapters may not create separate legal entities, bank accounts, logos, sponsorship packages, public policy positions, or clinical partnerships without national approval. Chapters must use approved waivers, training, brand materials, and reporting forms.

## Chapter Revocation

National leadership may suspend or revoke a chapter for safety violations, financial misconduct, discrimination, harassment, brand misuse, failure to report, unauthorized commitments, or conduct damaging to the mission. Emergency suspension may occur immediately when safety or legal exposure is present, with board review to follow.

## Expansion Readiness Checklist

Before national expansion accelerates, Lost Limb Riders should have adopted bylaws, insurance coverage, volunteer screening, chapter affiliation agreement, training modules, reporting forms, brand kit, financial controls, and incident response system.

# Lost Limb Riders Corporate Bylaws

## Article I — Name, Status, and Principal Office

### Section 1.01 — Name

The name of the corporation is Lost Limb Riders.

### Section 1.02 — Nonprofit Status

Lost Limb Riders is organized and shall be operated as a nonprofit corporation. The corporation intends to qualify, or maintain qualification if already recognized, as a tax‑exempt charitable organization under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code or the corresponding section of any future federal tax code.

### Section 1.03 — Principal Office

The principal office shall be located in Iowa at a location approved by the Board of Directors. The corporation may maintain additional offices, chapters, program sites, mailing addresses, and registered offices as approved by the Board.

### Iowa and Federal Review Note

Counsel should confirm the corporation’s legal name, registered office, registered agent, Iowa filings, charitable registration requirements, and federal tax‑exemption status before adoption.

## Article II — Purpose, Mission, and Powers

### Section 2.01 — Mission

Lost Limb Riders supports amputees, people with disabilities, veterans, first responders, and their families through peer support, education, advocacy, community engagement, family support, adaptive recreation, and motorcycle‑related fellowship.

### Section 2.02 — Charitable and Educational Purpose

The corporation is organized exclusively for charitable and educational purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3). The corporation shall not carry on activities that are not permitted to be carried on by an organization exempt under Section 501(c)(3).

### Section 2.03 — Powers

The corporation may exercise all powers available to an Iowa nonprofit corporation, subject to its Articles of Incorporation, these Bylaws, adopted policies, donor restrictions, and applicable law. These powers include receiving donations, making grants consistent with mission, conducting programs, entering contracts, employing staff, purchasing insurance, holding property, creating committees, developing chapters, and taking lawful actions necessary to advance the mission.

### Iowa and Federal Review Note

Counsel should confirm that the purpose and powers language is consistent with the Articles of Incorporation, Iowa Code Chapter 504, and IRS organizational test requirements for charitable organizations.

## Article III — Membership

### Section 3.01 — Non‑Membership Corporation

Lost Limb Riders shall be governed as a non‑membership corporation unless the Board and Articles of Incorporation expressly create statutory voting members. Program participants, volunteers, donors, riders, family members, supporters, advisory board members, and chapter participants may be called members for community purposes, but they shall not have statutory voting rights unless the Board formally amends governing documents to create such rights.

### Section 3.02 — Community Participants

The Board may establish classes of supporters, participants, riders, volunteers, donors, and chapter affiliates. These community designations do not create ownership, voting rights, fiduciary rights, or authority to bind the corporation.

### Iowa Review Note

Counsel should confirm that non‑membership status is consistent with the Articles of Incorporation and Iowa law. If statutory members are later created, the bylaws must be amended before implementation.

## Article IV — Board of Directors

### Section 4.01 — Authority

The Board of Directors is the governing body of Lost Limb Riders. The Board holds ultimate authority for mission, strategy, financial stewardship, legal compliance, executive accountability, risk oversight, policy adoption, and protection of charitable assets.

### Section 4.02 — Composition

The Board shall have not fewer than five and not more than eleven voting directors. The recommended launch size is seven voting directors.

### Section 4.03 — Qualifications

Directors must be at least eighteen years old, support the mission, act independently, complete orientation, sign the Board Member Agreement, disclose conflicts, maintain confidentiality, and comply with adopted policies. The Board should seek directors with lived experience with limb loss or disability, nonprofit governance, finance, healthcare, rehabilitation, veterans services, first responder services, legal, fundraising, safety, risk management, community leadership, or motorcycle safety experience.

### Section 4.04 — Election

Directors shall be elected by majority vote of the current voting directors at the annual meeting or at another meeting properly noticed for director election. The Governance Committee shall manage nominations and confirm eligibility before presentation to the Board.

### Section 4.05 — Terms

Directors shall serve two‑year terms unless otherwise specified at election. Terms should be staggered to preserve continuity. A director may serve up to three consecutive full terms. The Board may approve an exception when continuity, specialized expertise, or founder transition requires it.

### Section 4.06 — Vacancies

A vacancy may be filled by majority vote of the remaining directors. A director elected to fill a vacancy serves the remainder of the unexpired term unless the Board specifies a shorter transitional term.

### Section 4.07 — Resignation

A director may resign by written notice to the Chairperson or Secretary. Resignation is effective upon receipt unless the notice states a later date.

### Section 4.08 — Removal

A director may be removed for cause by two‑thirds vote of the current directors after notice and an opportunity to respond. Cause includes failure to attend meetings, breach of fiduciary duty, violation of policy, undisclosed conflict, misconduct, harassment, discrimination, misuse of assets, criminal conduct affecting the organization, or behavior that materially harms the mission.

### Iowa Review Note

Counsel should confirm director number, terms, removal procedures, notice requirements, and voting thresholds under Iowa nonprofit corporation law and the Articles of Incorporation.

## Article V — Officers and Executive Leadership

### Section 5.01 — Officers

The officers are Chairperson, Vice Chairperson, Secretary, Treasurer, and any additional officers approved by the Board.

### Section 5.02 — Election and Term

Officers shall be elected annually by the Board and may be reelected. Officers serve until a successor is elected, resignation, removal, or expiration of term.

### Section 5.03 — Chairperson

The Chairperson leads the Board, presides at meetings, supports agenda development, ensures committee accountability, coordinates executive evaluation, and represents the Board when authorized. The Chairperson does not manage daily operations unless separately appointed to an executive role by the Board.

### Section 5.04 — Vice Chairperson

The Vice Chairperson supports the Chairperson, performs Chairperson duties when needed, leads special governance assignments, and supports board recruitment and accountability.

### Section 5.05 — Secretary

The Secretary oversees notices, minutes, resolutions, corporate records, policy archives, board rosters, attendance records, and governance documentation.

### Section 5.06 — Treasurer

The Treasurer oversees financial governance, chairs or supports the Finance Committee, reviews financial reports, monitors internal controls, supports budget development, and reports financial matters to the Board.

### Section 5.07 — Executive Director

The Executive Director, if appointed, manages daily operations under Board authority. The Executive Director hires and supervises staff within approved budgets, implements programs, manages partnerships, supports fundraising, reports to the Board, and executes policies.

### Section 5.08 — Founder and Chief Executive Officer Role

The Founder/CEO role recognizes the founding leadership, public voice, relationship history, and mission stewardship of Lost Limb Riders. If the Founder/CEO is an employee or contracted executive, the role reports to the Board through an approved position description, compensation arrangement, conflict review, and annual evaluation. If the Founder/CEO is a board officer, board fiduciary duties control. The title does not create ownership of the nonprofit, unilateral authority over charitable assets, or authority to override Board decisions.

### Iowa and Federal Review Note

Counsel and a tax advisor should review officer structure, executive compensation, founder role, private benefit limits, and any related‑party arrangements.

## Article VI — Committees and Advisory Bodies

### Section 6.01 — Standing Committees

Standing committees are Executive, Finance, Governance, Audit when applicable, Fundraising, Programs, Safety and Risk, and Membership.

### Section 6.02 — Temporary Committees

The Board may create temporary committees or task forces for defined projects. Temporary committees expire when their assignment is complete unless renewed by the Board.

### Section 6.03 — Committee Authority

Committees advise the Board unless authority is expressly delegated by Board resolution, these Bylaws, or a committee charter. No committee may amend bylaws, approve dissolution, remove directors, approve merger, approve annual budgets unless delegated by law and policy, or take actions reserved to the Board.

### Section 6.04 — Executive Committee

The Executive Committee may act between Board meetings only on urgent matters within authority delegated by the Board. All Executive Committee actions must be reported to the Board at the next meeting and recorded in minutes. The Executive Committee may not replace full Board oversight.

### Section 6.05 — Advisory Board

The Board may appoint an Advisory Board of non‑voting advisors, including amputees, veterans, first responders, clinicians, safety experts, donors, community leaders, and family advocates. Advisory Board members provide advice, connections, and subject‑matter insight but do not vote as directors and do not bind the corporation.

### Iowa Review Note

Counsel should confirm committee delegation limits and any statutory restrictions on executive committee authority.

## Article VII — Meetings, Notice, Quorum, and Voting

### Section 7.01 — Regular Meetings

The Board shall meet at least quarterly. Monthly meetings are recommended during launch, major expansion, capital campaigns, or periods of increased risk.

### Section 7.02 — Annual Meeting

The annual meeting shall include election of directors and officers as needed, approval of strategic priorities, review of annual financial reports, committee appointments, policy review calendar, and conflict disclosure renewal.

### Section 7.03 — Special Meetings

Special meetings may be called by the Chairperson, Executive Committee, or any three voting directors. Notice must state the date, time, location or electronic platform, and purpose.

### Section 7.04 — Notice

Regular meeting materials should be provided at least five calendar days in advance. Special meeting notice should be provided at least forty‑eight hours in advance when practicable. Emergency meetings may be held with shorter notice when delay would materially harm the organization.

### Section 7.05 — Quorum

A majority of current voting directors constitutes quorum.

### Section 7.06 — Voting

Each director has one vote. Routine actions require majority vote of directors present after quorum is established. Major actions require two‑thirds vote of current directors unless a higher threshold is required by law or the Articles of Incorporation. Major actions include bylaw amendments, merger, dissolution, removal of directors, hiring or removal of the Executive Director, annual budget approval, real estate transactions, loans, chapter charter revocation, and obligations over thresholds set by policy.

### Section 7.07 — Electronic Meetings

The Board may meet by telephone, video conference, or other electronic means if all participating directors can hear one another or communicate substantially concurrently, participate in discussion, and vote in a manner that can be recorded.

### Section 7.08 — Action Without Meeting

Action without a meeting may be taken only when permitted by Iowa law and the Articles of Incorporation. Written consents shall be filed with corporate records.

## Harassment Policy

### Purpose

This policy ensures that Lost Limb Riders maintains an environment free from harassment, intimidation, bullying, exploitation, and abusive conduct for all participants, volunteers, staff, board members, partners, and community members.

### Scope

All organizational activities, programs, events, rides, meetings, communications, digital platforms, hospital visits, mentorship interactions, volunteer service, and any context in which individuals represent or engage with Lost Limb Riders.

### Definitions

**Harassment** includes unwelcome conduct based on race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, national origin, ancestry, age, disability, veteran status, genetic information, marital status, citizenship status, or any other protected characteristic. Harassment includes verbal, physical, visual, written, or electronic conduct that is sufficiently severe or pervasive to create a hostile, intimidating, or abusive environment.

**Sexual harassment** includes unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature, including quid pro quo and hostile environment harassment.

**Bullying** includes repeated, unreasonable actions directed at an individual or group that create a risk to health, safety, dignity, or well-being, including verbal abuse, threats, intimidation, humiliation, social exclusion, or sabotage.

**Exploitation** includes using an individual's vulnerability, personal story, medical information, or emotional state for personal gain, public attention, fundraising without consent, or any purpose inconsistent with the mission.

**Abusive conduct** includes conduct that a reasonable person would find hostile, threatening, violent, or demeaning, including bullying, yelling, insults, cruelty, intimidation, or retaliation.

### Policy Statement

Lost Limb Riders prohibits harassment, sexual harassment, bullying, exploitation, and abusive conduct by any person engaged in organizational activities. This prohibition applies to all relationships: board-to-staff, staff-to-volunteer, volunteer-to-participant, peer-to-peer, and any other interaction occurring in organizational context.

The motorcycle culture at the heart of Lost Limb Riders is built on brotherhood and sisterhood, mutual respect, and protection of one another. This policy reinforces — not contradicts — that culture. Brotherhood and sisterhood are not excuses for inappropriate conduct. They are commitments to treat one another with dignity.

### Procedures

**Reporting:** Any person who experiences, witnesses, or becomes aware of harassment, bullying, exploitation, or abusive conduct should report it to the Chairperson, Vice Chairperson, Executive Director, or Governance Committee chair. Reports may be made verbally or in writing. The organization shall accept and investigate anonymous reports when practical.

**Investigation:** The Board or designated leadership shall investigate reports promptly, thoroughly, and fairly. Investigations shall protect the privacy of all parties to the extent possible. Interim measures (such as temporary separation of parties) may be implemented during investigation when necessary to protect safety.

**Findings and Action:** Upon completion of investigation, the Board or designee shall determine whether policy was violated and take appropriate corrective action, which may include coaching, warning, restriction of duties, removal from events, suspension, termination, removal from board or volunteer service, or referral to authorities.

**Retaliation:** Retaliation against any person who reports harassment, participates in an investigation, or opposes conduct they reasonably believe violates this policy is strictly prohibited and is itself grounds for corrective action.

**External Context:** This policy also applies to organizational representatives at external events, partner facilities, and any context where the individual's connection to Lost Limb Riders is known or reasonably apparent.

### Responsibilities

All leaders must model respectful conduct and address observed violations promptly. The Board oversees complaints involving directors, officers, executives, or serious risk. Executive leadership oversees operational complaints. Every covered person is responsible for their own conduct and for reporting concerns.

## Safety and Risk Committee Charter

### Purpose

To oversee participant safety, motorcycle event risk, volunteer risk, insurance coordination, incident review, emergency planning, and risk culture.

### Authority

May recommend event suspension when safety risks are unacceptable. Emergency authority may be delegated by the Board or executive leadership for immediate safety decisions.

### Membership

Directors, ride safety leaders, risk professionals, healthcare advisors, insurance advisors, veterans or first responder advisors, and experienced riders as approved.

### Responsibilities

Review ride standards, participant releases, incident reports, insurance needs, emergency plans, volunteer screening, safety training, accessibility risks, and corrective actions.

### Meeting Expectations

Meets at least quarterly and before major motorcycle‑related events.

### Reporting Obligations

Reports incidents, risk trends, insurance gaps, and safety recommendations to the Board.

## Membership Committee Charter

### Purpose

To build an inclusive community of participants, riders, families, volunteers, donors, chapters, and supporters.

### Authority

Recommends membership categories, community standards, onboarding processes, and chapter affiliation standards.

### Membership

Directors, volunteers, participants, family representatives, chapter leaders, and staff as approved.

### Responsibilities

Coordinate onboarding, community engagement, retention, member communications, chapter support, volunteer pathways, and feedback loops.

### Meeting Expectations

Meets at least quarterly.

### Reporting Obligations

Reports membership trends, concerns, chapter needs, and inclusion barriers to the Board.
