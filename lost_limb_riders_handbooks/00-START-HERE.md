## Welcome to the Lost Limb Riders Organizational Handbooks

This directory contains all official handbooks, operational manuals, governance documents, and forms for Lost Limb Riders. The documentation is organized into four primary sections to serve different audiences and purposes.

---

## Directory Structure

### 1. Organization Handbook (Internal)
**Directory:** `01-Organization-Handbook/`  
**Audience:** Board Members, Executive Leadership, Senior Staff  
**Primary File:** `00-ORGANIZATION-HANDBOOK.md`

**Contains:**
- Mission Statement & Three-Tier Framework
- Corporate Bylaws & Legal Foundation
- Governance Manual & Board Operations
- Board Member Handbook & Orientation Guide
- Governance Policies & Committee Charters
- Administrative Operations Manual
- Financial Procedures & Controls
- Position Descriptions & Organizational Structure
- Safety & Risk Management Framework

**Purpose:** Serves as the authoritative internal reference for organizational governance, policies, procedures, and leadership responsibilities.

**Word Count:** ~16,000 words

---

### 2. Lost Limb Riders Member Handbook (Public)
**Directory:** `02-Member-Handbook/`  
**Audience:** Members, Participants, Volunteers, General Public  
**Primary File:** `00-MEMBER-HANDBOOK.md`

**Contains:**
- Organization mission, vision, and values
- Program overview and descriptions
- Member benefits and community standards
- Getting started guide
- Safety guidelines
- Volunteer opportunities
- Contact information and resources

**Purpose:** Provides members and the public with essential information about the organization, its programs, and how to get involved.

**Word Count:** ~8,700 words

---

### 3. Program Operational Manuals (Internal)
**Directory:** `03-Program-Manuals/`  
**Index File:** `00-PROGRAM-MANUALS-INDEX.md`

**Contains Six Individual Program Manuals:**

1. **Peer Connection Program** — Hospital visits and new amputee support (~17,900 words)
2. **Ride Forward Program** — Adaptive motorcycle riding and events (~14,100 words)
3. **Hospital & Prosthetic Outreach Program** — Provider partnerships (~34,300 words)
4. **Housing & Independence Advocacy Program** — Housing and accessibility resources (~11,700 words)
5. **Community Resource & Assistance Program** — Prosthetics and equipment assistance (~5,000 words)
6. **Family Support Program** — Family education and caregiver resources (~4,600 words)

**Purpose:** Detailed operational procedures, volunteer guidelines, safety protocols, and quality standards for each major program.

**Word Count:** ~83,000+ words total

---

### 4. Forms & Templates
**Directory:** `04-Forms-and-Templates/`  
**Index File:** `00-FORMS-INDEX.md`

**Contains:**
- Participant Release of Liability waiver
- Documentation Style Guide
- Index of forms with usage guidelines
- Placeholder documentation for additional required forms

**Purpose:** Centralized location for all official forms, waivers, and templates used across the organization.

**Word Count:** ~15,000+ words

## How to Navigate These Handbooks

### For Board Members & Executive Leadership
1. Start with `01-Organization-Handbook/00-ORGANIZATION-HANDBOOK.md`
2. Review relevant sections based on your committee or role
3. Reference program manuals for operational context

### For Program Staff & Coordinators
1. Review `02-Member-Handbook/` for public-facing program information
2. Review `03-Program-Manuals/00-PROGRAM-MANUALS-INDEX.md` to find your program manual
3. Reference `04-Forms-and-Templates/` for required forms and documentation

### For Volunteers
1. Start with `02-Member-Handbook/` for organization overview
2. Find your program in `03-Program-Manuals/` for volunteer guidelines
3. Complete required forms from `04-Forms-and-Templates/`

### For New Members/Participants
1. Read `02-Member-Handbook/00-MEMBER-HANDBOOK.md`
2. Complete required forms from `04-Forms-and-Templates/`
3. Contact the Program Coordinator for your program of interest

## Document Maintenance & Updates

All handbooks and manuals should be reviewed annually and updated as:
- Policies change
- Programs evolve
- New procedures are established
- Legal requirements shift

**Responsibility:** Executive Director and Board Governance Committee

**Update Process:** Changes to governance or major policies require Board approval. Changes to operational procedures require Program Director approval.
