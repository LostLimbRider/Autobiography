# A Letter to a New Amputee

Brother or Sister,

I know what you're feeling right now. I've been there.

You're looking at what's left and wondering how you're supposed to live the rest of your life like this. You're scared. You're angry. You're tired. And you're probably tired of people telling you it's going to be okay.

I'm not going to tell you it's going to be okay. I'm going to tell you something better: you're going to make it okay.

The first few months are the hardest. The phantom pain, the socket fittings that never feel right, the falls, the frustration. It's going to suck. I'm not going to lie to you.

But here's what I know: it gets better.

One day you'll stand without holding onto anything. One day you'll take a step without thinking about it. One day you'll forget you're wearing a prosthetic until you bump into something and remember.

And one day, hopefully sooner than you think, you'll laugh about it.

I joke that my stump looks like a giant penis. That's my way of taking the power back. Find your way. It might be humor. It might be something else. But find it.

Also, find your people. Find other amputees. They'll tell you the things the doctors won't. They'll show you what's possible. For me, that was the Lost Limb Riders. For you, it might be something else. But find them. You need them, and they need you.

You can do this. I promise you.

I Can. I Will.

So can you.

— John Thompson
