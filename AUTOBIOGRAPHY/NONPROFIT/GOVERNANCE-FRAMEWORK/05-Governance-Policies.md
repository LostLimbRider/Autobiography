# Lost Limb Riders Governance Policies

## Conflict of Interest Policy

### Purpose

This policy protects decision making from personal, family, financial, employment, political, or organizational interests that could affect judgment.

### Scope

This policy applies to directors, officers, committee members, employees, contractors, advisors, and key volunteers.

### Definitions

A conflict exists when a covered person’s interest could influence, or appear to influence, decisions for Lost Limb Riders. A related party includes family members, domestic partners, employers, businesses, close associates, and organizations in which the person has authority or financial interest.

### Policy Statement

Covered persons must disclose actual, potential, and perceived conflicts annually and whenever they arise. A conflicted person may provide factual information if requested but shall not vote and shall leave discussion when directed.

### Procedures

The Secretary maintains annual disclosures. The disinterested directors determine whether a conflict exists, whether the transaction is fair and reasonable, whether alternatives exist, and whether approval serves the organization’s best interests. Minutes must record disclosure, recusal, discussion, vote, and basis for approval.

### Responsibilities

Covered persons disclose. The Chairperson enforces meeting procedures. The Secretary records. The Board decides.

### Compliance Expectations

Failure to disclose may result in corrective action, removal, contract termination, or other remedies.

### Review Schedule

Review annually.

### Revision History

Version 1.0 — Initial board review draft — July 22, 2026.

## Code of Ethics

### Purpose

This policy defines ethical conduct for everyone representing Lost Limb Riders.

### Scope

Directors, officers, employees, volunteers, committee members, advisors, contractors, and chapter leaders.

### Policy Statement

Covered persons shall act honestly, comply with law and policy, protect participant dignity, use assets only for mission purposes, avoid exploitation, avoid private benefit, protect confidential information, report concerns, and treat people with respect.

### Procedures

Concerns may be reported to the Chairperson, Executive Director, Governance Committee, or Whistleblower contact. Reported concerns shall be reviewed promptly and fairly.

### Responsibilities

Leadership models ethical conduct. Supervisors and committee chairs address violations. The Board addresses director and executive violations.

### Compliance Expectations

Violations may result in coaching, restriction of duties, removal from events, termination, or referral to authorities.

### Review Schedule

Review every two years or after major incidents.

### Revision History

Version 1.0 — Initial board review draft — July 22, 2026.

## Confidentiality Policy

### Purpose

This policy protects private information entrusted to Lost Limb Riders.

### Scope

All directors, officers, employees, volunteers, committee members, advisors, contractors, and chapter leaders.

### Definitions

Confidential information includes participant information, donor records, medical or disability information, personnel matters, investigations, legal matters, security information, account information, private family information, internal strategy, and nonpublic financial information.

### Policy Statement

Confidential information may be used only for authorized mission purposes. Confidentiality continues after service ends.

### Procedures

Access shall be limited to people with a need to know. Records shall be stored securely. Suspected disclosure must be reported immediately. Public storytelling requires consent when identifying private individuals or sensitive facts.

### Responsibilities

The Secretary oversees board records. Executive leadership oversees operational records. Every covered person protects information.

### Compliance Expectations

Unauthorized disclosure may result in removal, termination, loss of access, or legal action.

### Review Schedule

Review annually.

### Revision History

Version 1.0 — Initial board review draft — July 22, 2026.

## Whistleblower Policy

### Purpose

This policy provides safe reporting of suspected misconduct.

### Scope

Directors, officers, employees, volunteers, participants, contractors, donors, and community members.

### Policy Statement

Lost Limb Riders prohibits retaliation against anyone who makes a good‑faith report of suspected fraud, theft, abuse, harassment, discrimination, safety violations, financial misconduct, conflicts, records destruction, or legal violations.

### Procedures

Reports may be made to the Chairperson, Vice Chairperson, Executive Director, Governance Committee chair, or another designated reporting contact. Reports may be anonymous when practical. The Board or designee shall review reports promptly, protect confidentiality as much as possible, document findings, and take corrective action.

### Responsibilities

All leaders must receive reports seriously. The Board oversees reports involving directors, officers, executives, finance, or serious risk.

### Compliance Expectations

Retaliation is grounds for removal or termination.

### Review Schedule

Review annually.

### Revision History

Version 1.0 — Initial board review draft — July 22, 2026.

## Document Retention and Destruction Policy

### Purpose

This policy ensures records are preserved, retrievable, and destroyed only when appropriate.

### Scope

All organizational records in paper, electronic, audio, video, image, message, and database formats.

### Policy Statement

Records shall be retained according to approved schedules and legal holds. No record may be destroyed to conceal wrongdoing, avoid disclosure, or interfere with investigation, audit, litigation, or regulatory review.

### Procedures

Permanent records include Articles, bylaws, minutes, board resolutions, tax exemption letters, major contracts, property records, annual financial statements, and policies. Financial records should generally be retained at least seven years unless counsel or a CPA recommends longer. Personnel records, participant records, donor records, insurance records, incident reports, and grant records shall follow schedules approved by counsel and operational policy. Legal holds suspend destruction.

### Responsibilities

The Secretary oversees governance records. Executive leadership oversees operational records. Finance maintains financial records. Program leaders maintain program records.

### Compliance Expectations

Improper destruction may result in corrective action or legal referral.

### Review Schedule

Review annually with counsel and CPA input.

### Revision History

Version 1.0 — Initial board review draft — July 22, 2026.

## Records Management Policy

### Purpose

This policy establishes accurate, secure, accessible, and organized records management.

### Scope

All official records of Lost Limb Riders.

### Policy Statement

Records must be accurate, timely, secure, backed up when electronic, and available to authorized users. Drafts should not be treated as final approvals.

### Procedures

Adopted documents shall be marked with approval dates. Minutes shall record motions, votes, recusals, abstentions, and action items. Electronic files shall use consistent names and restricted access for sensitive information. Official records shall be stored in approved systems.

### Responsibilities

The Secretary maintains corporate records. Department leads maintain department files. The Board reviews record practices annually.

### Compliance Expectations

Unauthorized alteration, removal, or concealment of records is prohibited.

### Review Schedule

Review annually.

### Revision History

Version 1.0 — Initial board review draft — July 22, 2026.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Policy

### Purpose

This policy ensures people with limb loss, limb difference, disability, medical needs, trauma history, family caregiving roles, and different communication needs can participate with dignity.

### Scope

Programs, rides, meetings, events, facilities, communications, digital content, volunteer roles, and chapters.

### Policy Statement

Lost Limb Riders shall plan for accessibility, reasonable accommodations, inclusive communication, safe participation, and respectful treatment. Motorcycle participation shall be governed by safety standards, lawful licensing, insurance, event rules, and individual readiness; inclusion does not require unsafe participation.

### Procedures

Event planners shall assess accessible parking, restrooms, seating, routes, emergency access, communication formats, caregiver participation, service animals, sensory needs, and mobility needs. Accommodation requests shall be reviewed promptly. Denials must be based on safety, undue burden, fundamental alteration, or legal limitation and should be documented.

### Responsibilities

The Programs Committee and Safety and Risk Committee coordinate accessibility planning. Executive leadership implements accommodations. The Board reviews barriers annually.

### Compliance Expectations

Disrespect, exclusion without review, or retaliation for accommodation requests is prohibited.

### Review Schedule

Review annually with participant feedback.

### Revision History

Version 1.0 — Initial board review draft — July 22, 2026.
