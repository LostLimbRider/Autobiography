# Lost Limb Riders Board Member Handbook

## Welcome

Serving on the Lost Limb Riders Board means protecting a mission built around people who have already fought through enough. Board service is not honorary. It is active stewardship of money, safety, dignity, trust, and community.

## Board Member Duties

Directors owe duties of care, loyalty, and obedience. Care means paying attention and making informed decisions. Loyalty means putting the organization ahead of personal interests. Obedience means following the mission, governing documents, donor restrictions, and applicable law.

## Expectations

Board members shall attend meetings, read materials, serve on at least one committee, make a personally meaningful annual contribution, help open doors for fundraising, protect confidential information, disclose conflicts, support adopted decisions, and speak respectfully about the organization.

## Meeting Standards

Directors should arrive prepared, ask direct questions, focus on governance instead of micromanagement, respect time limits, document decisions through motions, and avoid side deals or unofficial commitments.

## Financial Stewardship

Directors review budgets, financial reports, restricted funds, internal controls, fundraising performance, insurance, and audit or review needs. Directors must ask questions when numbers are unclear.

## Risk Stewardship

Because Lost Limb Riders may involve motorcycle‑related activities, vulnerable participants, volunteers, public events, families, veterans, first responders, and people with disabilities, the Board must treat safety and risk as core governance work.

## Communications

No director may speak for the Board unless authorized. Directors may share public information and mission stories, but must not disclose private participant, donor, personnel, medical, legal, or investigation information.

## Board Code of Conduct

Directors shall act with honesty, respect, accountability, inclusion, and courage. Directors shall not harass, discriminate, retaliate, exploit their role, misuse assets, accept improper benefits, undermine adopted decisions, or make unauthorized commitments.

## Board Removal Acknowledgment

A director who violates bylaws, policies, confidentiality, conflict rules, safety standards, or fiduciary duties may be subject to corrective action or removal under the bylaws.
