# Lost Limb Riders Committee Charters

## Executive Committee Charter

### Purpose

To handle urgent governance matters between Board meetings and coordinate board workflow.

### Authority

Acts only within authority delegated by the Board or bylaws. It may not amend bylaws, approve dissolution, remove directors, approve merger, approve annual budget unless expressly allowed, or replace full Board oversight.

### Membership

Chairperson, Vice Chairperson, Secretary, Treasurer, and committee chairs or at‑large directors appointed by the Board.

### Responsibilities

Set board agendas, coordinate executive evaluation, monitor urgent risks, recommend board actions, and act between meetings when delay would harm the organization.

### Meeting Expectations

Meets as needed. Emergency decisions must be documented.

### Reporting Obligations

Reports all actions at the next Board meeting for ratification or acknowledgement.

## Finance Committee Charter

### Purpose

To strengthen financial stewardship, budgeting, reporting, internal controls, and long‑term sustainability.

### Authority

Recommends budgets and financial policies. May approve expenditures only within limits delegated by the Board.

### Membership

Treasurer as chair or liaison, at least two directors, and non‑director advisors with finance experience when approved.

### Responsibilities

Review budgets, financial statements, cash flow, restricted funds, internal controls, investment practices, insurance cost planning, tax filings, and financial procedures.

### Meeting Expectations

Meets at least quarterly and more often during budget season.

### Reporting Obligations

Provides financial reports and recommendations to the Board.

## Governance Committee Charter

### Purpose

To maintain strong board recruitment, nominations, orientation, evaluation, bylaws, policies, and governance culture.

### Authority

Recommends governance actions. Does not elect or remove directors without Board action.

### Membership

At least three directors. Non‑director advisors may support recruitment or training.

### Responsibilities

Manage board nominations, skills matrix, orientation, annual self‑assessment, policy review calendar, bylaw review, officer succession, and board accountability.

### Meeting Expectations

Meets at least quarterly.

### Reporting Obligations

Reports nominations, policy updates, and governance concerns to the Board.

## Audit Committee Charter

### Purpose

To provide independent oversight of audits, reviews, financial integrity, fraud risk, and whistleblower matters involving finance.

### Authority

Oversees external audit or review when required or approved. Recommends auditor engagement to the Board.

### Membership

At least three directors, preferably independent of day‑to‑day finance handling. Treasurer may participate but should not chair when independence is needed.

### Responsibilities

Recommend audit or review scope, meet with CPA, review findings, monitor corrective actions, oversee fraud reports, and review Form 990 before filing when applicable.

### Meeting Expectations

Meets when an audit, review, major financial concern, or Form 990 review requires it.

### Reporting Obligations

Reports findings and corrective action status to the Board.

## Fundraising Committee Charter

### Purpose

To support ethical fundraising, donor stewardship, sponsorships, grants, campaigns, and community partnerships.

### Authority

Recommends fundraising plans and donor policies. Cannot accept restricted gifts that conflict with mission or create unacceptable risk without Board approval.

### Membership

Directors, staff, volunteers, donors, and community advisors approved by the Board or executive leadership.

### Responsibilities

Develop annual fundraising plan, support events, identify prospects, steward donors, review sponsorship opportunities, protect donor intent, and coordinate with communications.

### Meeting Expectations

Meets at least quarterly and more often around campaigns.

### Reporting Obligations

Reports fundraising progress, risks, restricted gifts, and campaign needs to the Board.

## Programs Committee Charter

### Purpose

To oversee mission quality, participant benefit, program evaluation, family support, education, advocacy, and community engagement.

### Authority

Recommends program priorities, evaluation measures, and resource needs. Program operations remain with executive leadership unless delegated.

### Membership

Directors, participants, family representatives, program leaders, clinicians, veterans or first responder advisors, and volunteers as approved.

### Responsibilities

Review program design, participant feedback, accessibility, outcomes, partnerships, family support resources, and alignment with mission.

### Meeting Expectations

Meets at least quarterly.

### Reporting Obligations

Reports program performance, participant needs, and recommended improvements to the Board.

## Safety and Risk Committee Charter

### Purpose

To oversee participant safety, motorcycle event risk, volunteer risk, insurance coordination, incident review, emergency planning, and risk culture.

### Authority

May recommend event suspension when safety risks are unacceptable. Emergency authority may be delegated by the Board or executive leadership for immediate safety decisions.

### Membership

Directors, ride safety leaders, risk professionals, healthcare advisors, insurance advisors, veterans or first responder advisors, and experienced riders as approved.

### Responsibilities

Review ride standards, participant releases, incident reports, insurance needs, emergency plans, volunteer screening, safety training, accessibility risks, and corrective actions.

### Meeting Expectations

Meets at least quarterly and before major motorcycle‑related events.

### Reporting Obligations

Reports incidents, risk trends, insurance gaps, and safety recommendations to the Board.

## Membership Committee Charter

### Purpose

To build an inclusive community of participants, riders, families, volunteers, donors, chapters, and supporters.

### Authority

Recommends membership categories, community standards, onboarding processes, and chapter affiliation standards.

### Membership

Directors, volunteers, participants, family representatives, chapter leaders, and staff as approved.

### Responsibilities

Coordinate onboarding, community engagement, retention, member communications, chapter support, volunteer pathways, and feedback loops.

### Meeting Expectations

Meets at least quarterly.

### Reporting Obligations

Reports membership trends, concerns, chapter needs, and inclusion barriers to the Board.
