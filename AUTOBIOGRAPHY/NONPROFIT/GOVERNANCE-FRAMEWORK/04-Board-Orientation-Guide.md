# Lost Limb Riders Board Orientation Guide

## Orientation Purpose

Orientation prepares each director to govern Lost Limb Riders with confidence, consistency, legal awareness, financial discipline, and respect for the people served.

## Orientation Packet

Each new director shall receive the Corporate Bylaws, Governance Manual, Board Member Handbook, Board Member Agreement, Committee Charters, current budget, most recent financial reports, strategic plan if adopted, insurance summary, program summary, chapter standards if adopted, conflict disclosure form, and core policies.

## First 30 Days

New directors shall meet with the Chairperson, Executive Director or Founder/CEO, Treasurer, and assigned committee chair. They shall review governing documents, complete conflict disclosure, sign required acknowledgments, and attend an orientation session.

## First 60 Days

New directors shall join at least one committee, review financial reports with the Treasurer if needed, understand safety and risk obligations, and complete nonprofit governance training recommended by the Governance Committee.

## First 90 Days

New directors shall participate in a board meeting, contribute to committee work, identify fundraising or partnership connections, and complete any remaining training.

## Required Acknowledgments

Each director must sign the Board Member Agreement, Conflict of Interest Disclosure, Confidentiality Acknowledgment, Code of Ethics Acknowledgment, Board Code of Conduct Acknowledgment, and Whistleblower Policy Acknowledgment.
