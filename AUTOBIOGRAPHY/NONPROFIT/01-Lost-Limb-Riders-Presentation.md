# Lost Limb Riders — Nonprofit Presentation

## Presentation for Potential Donors, Sponsors, and Community Partners

**Runtime:** 10–15 minutes (with optional Q&A)
**Presented by:** John Thompson

---

### I. Opening (2 min)

"Most people think losing a limb is the end of the world. It's not. It's the beginning of a different world. And in that world, you need people who understand what you're going through. That's where the Lost Limb Riders come in."

---

### II. The Problem (3 min)

- 185,000+ amputations occur in the US each year
- Amputees face higher rates of depression, isolation, and suicide
- The mental and emotional recovery is often harder than the physical
- Many new amputees never meet another amputee during their recovery
- The healthcare system treats the wound, not the person

---

### III. The Solution — Lost Limb Riders (4 min)

**Who we are:**
A community of amputees and supporters who ride motorcycles, support each other, and show new amputees what's possible.

**What we do:**
- Peer visits to new amputees in hospitals and rehab centers
- Group rides and events that build community
- Mentorship program — matching new amputees with experienced ones
- Advocacy and awareness
- Fundraising for prosthetics and adaptive equipment

**Why it works:**
- Peer support is proven to improve outcomes
- Seeing someone who's been through it and is thriving changes everything
- The motorcycle aspect breaks down barriers — it's not a support group, it's a riding club

---

### IV. Impact Stories (3 min)

**John's Story:**
"I thought my life was over when I lost my leg. Then I connected with a group of bikers who took me in. They weren't amputees — they were just riders. But they didn't treat me like I was broken. They treated me like one of them. That changed everything."

### V. The Ask (2 min)

**What we need:**
- Financial support for prosthetics and equipment
- Volunteers for peer visits and events
- Partnerships with hospitals and rehab centers
- Sponsors for rides and events
- Help spreading the word

**What your support does:**
- A $500 donation provides a prosthetic component
- A $1,000 donation sponsors an amputee's first ride
- A $5,000 donation funds a year of peer visits

---

### VI. Closing (1 min)

"You don't have to lose a limb to be part of this community. You just have to care. And if you're sitting here today, I already know you do."

"Thank you for your time. Thank you for your support. And if you ever want to ride with us — you're welcome."

"I Can. I Will. We Can. We Will."
