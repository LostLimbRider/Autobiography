# Full Keynote — 40 Minutes
## I Can. I Will.
### By John Thompson

**Audience:** General — corporate events, conferences, fundraisers, large gatherings
**Tone:** Inspirational, honest, humorous, high-energy
**Runtime:** 35–40 minutes

---

### I. Opening (3 min)

Start with the punchline.

"Let me tell you something nobody warns you about: when you lose your leg below the knee, the stump looks like a giant penis. You either laugh about it or you drive yourself crazy. I chose to laugh."

Hook the audience with humor and honesty. Establish immediately that this won't be a pity story.

"I'm John Thompson. I've been knocked down more times than I can count. And I'm still standing. This is how."

---

### II. The Falls (10 min)

Briefly walk through each major hit:

- **Serious illness** — the first time life showed him he wasn't invincible
- **Addiction** — the slow creep, the rock bottom, the climb back
- **Loss of loved ones** — grief and learning to carry it
- **Heart attack** — when his own body turned on him
- **Cancer** — chemo, fear, and fighting through

Hit the highlights of each. Don't dwell. Keep it moving. The point is the accumulation — how each blow could have been the one that ended him.

"What I want you to understand is this: none of those things broke me. They bent me. They bent me so far I thought I'd snap. But I didn't."

---

### III. The Leg (7 min)

The amputation story.

Waking up. Looking down. The prosthetic. Learning to walk again. The falls. The frustration. The moment he realized he had to choose.

"Losing my leg was the hardest physical thing I've ever been through. And I'd been through a lot. But here's what I learned: your body adapts. Your mind adapts. You find a way or you don't. I decided I would find a way."

Transition to the humor:

"Still looks like a giant penis though. You get used to it."

---

### IV. The Support System (5 min)

- Genie — his wife who never left
- His daughters — why he kept fighting
- His dog Roxy — "scan, scan, scan"
- The Lost Limb Riders — finding his tribe

"The people who love you don't care if you're missing pieces. They care that you're still here. Make sure you're still here for them."

---

### V. Faith (3 min)

Brief and honest. Not preachy.

"I'm not here to convert anyone. But I know this: something carried me when I couldn't walk. Call it whatever you want. I call it God. And I'm grateful."

---

### VI. The Message (7 min)

The heart of the speech.

"I Can. I Will."

- Where it came from
- What it means
- How he uses it every day

"You don't wait until you feel ready. You start. The readiness follows. You don't wait until you believe. You act. And the belief catches up."

---

### VII. Call to Action (5 min)

Direct address to the audience.

"Look at the person next to you. They're fighting something you don't know about. We all are. So be kind. Be patient. Be the reason someone doesn't quit today."

"And if you're the one fighting — don't quit. I know it hurts. I know you're tired. I know you don't know if you can make it. But you can. You will. Because I did, and I'm nothing special. I'm just a guy who refused to stay down."

---

### VIII. Closing (30 sec)

"I Can. I Will."

"So can you."

"Thank you."

*(Exit to applause.)*
