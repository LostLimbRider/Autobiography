# Keynote Template — Nonprofit / Fundraiser
## Lost Limb Riders & Amputee Community
### By John Thompson

**Audience:** Nonprofit events, fundraising galas, charity runs, donor events
**Tone:** Inspirational, gratitude-focused, community-driven
**Runtime:** 15–25 minutes (adjust by trimming sections)

---

### Core Angle

Highlight **community, purpose, and the power of showing up for each other**. This is about what happens when people come together to support a cause.

---

### I. Opening (2 min)

"I lost my leg. I thought my life was over. Then I found a group of people who had also lost limbs — and they were out there riding motorcycles, laughing, living. That's when I realized: I wasn't broken. I was just different. And different can still be beautiful."

---

### II. Finding the Lost Limb Riders (5 min)

- How he heard about them
- The first meeting — the fear, the welcome, the relief
- The first ride — what it felt like to be free again
- The community that became family

---

### III. Why This Work Matters (5 min)

- The impact of peer support for new amputees
- The isolation that comes after amputation
- How one conversation can change everything
- The ripple effect — helping one person helps their whole family

---

### IV. Call to Support (3 min)

- What the organization does
- What donations fund
- Who it helps
- The difference between surviving and thriving

---

### V. Closing (2 min)

"Every person in this room has the power to change someone's life. Not by doing something dramatic. Just by showing up. By caring. By giving someone a reason to believe they can keep going."

"Thank you for being here. Thank you for caring. Let's keep riding."

"I Can. I Will."

"So can they."
