# Keynote Template — Physical Therapy Audience
## Fast Recovery / Rebuilding Strength
### By John Thompson

**Audience:** Physical therapy patients, rehab clinics, amputee support groups, PT conferences
**Tone:** Encouraging, practical, focused on the work
**Runtime:** 20–30 minutes (adjust by trimming sections)

---

### Core Angle

Highlight **recovery speed and determination**. These people are in the trenches right now. They need to see that the work pays off.

---

### I. Opening (2 min)

"I know what you're going through. I've been there. I remember my first time trying to stand on my prosthetic. I lasted about three seconds before I hit the floor. But I got back up. And I kept getting back up. That's all recovery is. Getting back up one more time than you fall."

---

### II. My Recovery Story (8 min)

Focus heavily on:

- The first days after amputation
- Learning to walk again — the falls, the frustration, the small victories
- Physical therapy sessions that felt impossible
- The moment he took his first real steps
- How he measured progress in inches before miles

"The first time I walked to the bathroom without help, I almost cried. Not because it was impressive. Because I knew how much work it took to get there. Every rep, every stretch, every painful step — it all added up."

---

### III. What Recovery Taught Me (5 min)

- Progress isn't linear — some days you go backward
- Pain is temporary; quitting is permanent
- You have to celebrate the small wins
- Your mindset matters more than your body

---

### IV. Practical Advice (5 min)

- Listen to your body but don't let it talk you out of trying
- Find a community of people who've been through it
- Laugh when you can
- Set micro-goals

---

### V. Closing (2 min)

"You're stronger than you think. I know that sounds like something people just say. But I've seen it. I've lived it. You will walk again. You will run again. You will live again. Just don't quit before you get there."

"I Can. I Will."

"So can you."
